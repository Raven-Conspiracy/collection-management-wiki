## 1.3 JP 3-0, Joint Campaigns and Operations

**Publication:** JP 3-0, Joint Campaigns and Operations  
**Date:** 18 June 2022  
**JCS Series Page:** https://www.jcs.mil/doctrine/joint-doctrine-pubs/3-0-operations-series/

### Core Concept
JP 3-0 establishes ISR as an integral element of all phases of joint operations. It defines CCIRs (including PIRs and FFIRs), the role of ISR in the operations process, and establishes that ISR synchronization and integration are key staff functions supporting command decision-making.

### Key Terms

**CCIR (Commander's Critical Information Requirement):**
> "A commander's critical information requirement is an information requirement identified by the commander as being critical to facilitating timely decisionmaking (JP 3-0)." — Cited in ATP 2-01 and FM 2-0

CCIRs have two sub-categories:
- **PIRs** — intelligence focus; driven by J-2/G-2/S-2
- **FFIRs (Friendly Force Information Requirements)** — friendly force focus; driven by J-3/G-3/S-3

> "A CCIR directly influences decisionmaking and facilitates the successful execution of military operations." — JP 3-0 (as cited in ATP 2-01)

**ISR in Operations:**  
JP 3-0 establishes that ISR supports all phases of joint operations (Shape, Deter, Seize Initiative, Dominate, Stabilize, Enable Civil Authority). ISR planning begins at the outset of operational planning and is synchronized with the OPLAN/CONPLAN through Annex B (Intelligence) and associated appendices.

> "Targeting is the process of selecting and prioritizing targets and matching the appropriate response to them, considering operational requirements and capabilities." — JP 3-0 (as cited in JP 3-04)
