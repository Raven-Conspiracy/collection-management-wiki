## 1.1 JP 2-0, Joint Intelligence

**Publication:** JP 2-0, Joint Intelligence  
**Current Status:** Keystone document for joint intelligence doctrine  
**Public URL:** https://www.benning.army.mil/infantry/doctrinesupplement/atp3-21.8/PDFs/jp2_0.pdf  
**JCS Series Page:** https://www.jcs.mil/Doctrine/Joint-Doctrine-Pubs/2-0-Intelligence-Series/

### Core Concept
JP 2-0 is the foundational doctrine for all joint intelligence activities. It establishes the six categories of the joint intelligence process and defines the intelligence disciplines (HUMINT, SIGINT, GEOINT, MASINT, OSINT, TECHINT). It defines collection management and its two complementary functions—CRM and COM—and anchors them in the concept of Collection Management Authority (CMA).

### The Joint Intelligence Process (Six Categories)
> "The six categories of intelligence operations are: planning and direction; collection; processing and exploitation; analysis and production; dissemination and integration; and evaluation and feedback." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Planning and Direction | Identifies intelligence requirements; develops collection strategy |
| Collection | Acquisition of data by tasked sensors and assets |
| Processing and Exploitation | Conversion of raw data into usable information |
| Analysis and Production | Integration and interpretation to produce finished intelligence |
| Dissemination and Integration | Delivery of intelligence to users |
| Evaluation and Feedback | Assessment of the process; retasking as needed |

### Key Terms from JP 2-0

**Collection Management:**
> "Collection management is the process of converting intelligence-related information requirements into collection requirements, establishing priorities, tasking or coordinating with appropriate collection sources or agencies, monitoring results, and retasking, as required." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8); Glossary Pt. II

**Collection Requirements Management (CRM):**
> "CRM is the authoritative development and control of collection, processing, exploitation, and information reporting requirements." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8)(a)

**Collection Operations Management (COM):**
> "COM is the authoritative direction, scheduling, and control of specific collection operations and associated processing, exploitation, and information reporting resources." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8)(b)

**Collection Management Authority (CMA):**
> "Anchored on the appropriate collection management authority (CMA), collection management is composed of two components, collection requirements management (CRM) and collection operations management (COM)." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8)

**Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR):**
> "An activity that synchronizes and integrates the planning and operation of sensors, assets, and processing, exploitation, and dissemination systems in direct support of current and future operations. This is an integrated intelligence and operations function." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(3)(b) [KEY TERM]

**Collection Plan:**
> "A systematic scheme to optimize the employment of all available collection capabilities and associated processing, exploitation, and dissemination resources to satisfy specific information requirements." — JP 2-0, Glossary Pt. II

**Collection Strategy:**
> "A scheme for collecting information from all available sources to satisfy SIRs." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8)(a); Glossary Pt. II

**Priority Intelligence Requirement (PIR):**
> "An intelligence requirement stated as a priority for intelligence support, that the commander and staff need to understand the adversary or operational environment." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(1); Glossary Pt. II

**Essential Elements of Information (EEI):**
> "The most critical information requirements regarding the adversary and the environment needed by the commander by a particular time to relate with other available information and intelligence in order to reach a logical decision." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(1); Glossary Pt. II

**Specific Information Requirement (SIR):**
> "A basic question that must be answered to satisfy a collection request." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(1)(f) [KEY TERM]; Glossary Pt. II

**JIPOE:**
> "JIPOE is the continuous process through which J-2 manages the analysis and development of products that help the commander and staff understand the complex and interconnected OE." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.d.(1)(a)

**JCMB:**
> "The joint force may establish a joint collection management board (JCMB) to monitor and update collection requirements and asset status, and recommends the revised collection plan for approval by the commander." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8)(e)

**Cross-Cueing:**
> "Multiple collection sources enable collection managers to cross-cue between different sources (e.g., using signals intelligence [SIGINT] direction finding to focus collection by geospatial intelligence [GEOINT] systems)." — JP 2-0, Ch. I, Sec. 3.a.(8)(d)

### Intelligence Disciplines (JP 2-0 Appendix B)
| Discipline | Definition (direct quote) |
|---|---|
| HUMINT | "A category of intelligence derived from information collected and provided by human sources." |
| SIGINT | "Intelligence produced by exploiting foreign communications systems and noncommunications emitters." (COMINT, ELINT, FISINT sub-categories) |
| GEOINT | "The exploitation and analysis of imagery and geospatial information to describe, assess, and visually depict physical features and geographically referenced activities on the Earth." |
| MASINT | "Information produced by quantitative and qualitative analysis of physical attributes of targets and events to characterize, locate, and identify them." |
| OSINT | "Intelligence based on open source information that any member of the public can lawfully obtain by request, purchase, or observation." |
| TECHINT | "Derived from the exploitation of foreign materiel and scientific information." |
