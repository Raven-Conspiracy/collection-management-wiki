## 1.4 JP 3-60, Joint Targeting

**Publication:** JP 3-60, Joint Targeting  
**Date:** 28 September 2018  
**Public URL:** https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/FOID/Reading%20Room/Joint_Staff/21-F-0520_JP_3-60_9-28-2018.pdf

### Core Concept
JP 3-60 governs the joint targeting cycle. The J-2's role in targeting is to prioritize and synchronize intelligence collection efforts in direct support of each phase of the six-phase targeting cycle, from target development through bomb damage assessment (BDA). Collection is essential at every phase.

### The Joint Targeting Cycle (Six Phases)

| Phase | Title | Intelligence/Collection Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Commander's Objectives, Targeting Guidance, and Intent | J-2 provides JIPOE, threat assessments, target system analysis |
| 2 | Target Development and Prioritization | J-2 develops target folders, HVT/HPT nominations, validates targets |
| 3 | Capabilities Analysis | J-2 assesses target vulnerabilities; collection required for BDA planning |
| 4 | Commander's Decision and Force Assignment | Collection resources identified and allocated |
| 5 | Mission Planning and Force Execution | Specific ISR tasking issued; pre-strike collection |
| 6 | Assessment | BDA collection; J-2 conducts physical damage assessment (PDA), functional damage assessment (FDA) |

### J-2 Role in Targeting

> "Intelligence Directorate of a Joint Staff (J-2). The J-2 prioritizes intelligence collection efforts, analysis, validation, and assessment for all joint operations. In addition, the J-2 provides a major input to the operations directorate of a joint staff (J-3) and plans directorate of a joint staff (J-5) in the form of adversary course of action assessments critical to the joint target prioritization process and identification of HVTs and HPTs." — JP 3-60, Executive Summary

> "Targeting integrates intelligence, plans, and operations across all commands within a joint force and during all phases of operations." — JP 3-60, Ch. I, Sec. B, Para. 4.b

> "Detailed foundational intelligence products (e.g., dynamic threat assessments, joint intelligence preparation of the operational environment, country assessments) facilitate detailed targeting, starting with target systems analysis." — JP 3-60, Executive Summary

> "Targeting leverages the commander's guidance and intent and the operational objectives from joint planning to identify the intelligence requirements for the joint intelligence preparation of the operational environment (JIPOE) and other products of the intelligence process." — JP 3-60, Ch. I, Sec. B, Para. 4.b

### F2T2EA (Find, Fix, Track, Target, Engage, Assess)

The F2T2EA sequence represents the kill-chain process for dynamic targeting. Collection management is central to the "Find," "Fix," and "Track" steps:
- **Find:** Collection strategy identifies potential targets; GEOINT, SIGINT, HUMINT identify adversary.
- **Fix:** Persistent surveillance and precise geolocation through ISR assets.
- **Track:** Continuous ISR management maintains custody of time-sensitive targets (TSTs).
- **Target/Engage/Assess:** Collection continues through engagement and post-strike BDA.

ISR assets allocated to the JIPCL support F2T2EA operations. The JCMB adjudicates competing requests for limited ISR assets between deliberate targeting and dynamic targeting requirements.
