## 3.1 AFDP 2-0, Intelligence

**Publication:** Air Force Doctrine Publication 2-0, Intelligence  
**Current Edition:** 1 May 2026 (most recent)  
**Public URL:** https://www.doctrine.af.mil/Portals/61/documents/AFDP_2-0/2-0-AFDP-INTELLIGENCE.pdf  
**Doctrine Page:** https://www.doctrine.af.mil/Doctrine-Publications/AFDP-2-0-Intelligence/

### Core Concept
AFDP 2-0 is the Air Force's foundational intelligence doctrine publication. It governs all Air Force ISR activities from planning through dissemination and specifically establishes the ISRD (ISR Division of the AOC) as the central node for Air Force collection management. It defines the Air Force-specific PCPADE intelligence process, CRM/COM functions, collection management authority, and how the Air Force interfaces with joint collection management structures.

### The PCPADE Process (Air Force Intelligence Process)

> "The USAF is the only service to refer to the joint intelligence process as an acronym (PCPADE)." — AFDP 2-0

| Step | Title |
|---|---|
| P | Planning and Direction |
| C | Collection |
| P | Processing and Exploitation |
| A | Analysis and Production |
| D | Dissemination and Integration |
| E | Evaluation and Feedback |

### Collection Management Authority in Air Force Context

> "At the theater level, CCDRs exercise collection management authority (CMA) for collection operations within their area of responsibility (AOR). CMA is the authority to establish, prioritize, and validate theater collection requirements, establish sensor tasking guidance, and develop theater-wide collection policies." — AFDP 2-0

> "Collection Management Authority (CMA). CMA is the authority to establish, prioritize, and validate theater collection requirements, establish sensor tasking guidance, and develop theater-wide collection policies. Commanders exercising OPCON over intelligence forces may assume CMA of tasked ISR assets as part of the delegation of authority." — AFDP 2-0

> "The theater J2 usually retains CMA to validate, modify, or non-concur theater intelligence requirements through the CCDR's joint intelligence operations center (JIOC)." — AFDP 2-0

> "The National Security Agency (NSA) retains CMA over strategic-capable SIGINT ISR systems. In addition, CCDRs may request, and subsequently receive, temporary SIGINT operational tasking authority (SOTA) over theater-wide capable platforms and sensors. The delegation of SOTA to a CCDR and subsequent delegation of this authority to the JFC ensures the theater can prioritize requirements and focus SIGINT collection where needed to carry out assigned missions." — AFDP 2-0

### CRM and COM (Air Force Application)

> "Collection management includes two complementary functions: collection requirements management (CRM) and collection operations management (COM). Collection Requirements Management (CRM). CRM is the authoritative development and control of collection, processing, exploitation, and information reporting requirements. CRM defines what intelligence systems should collect. Collection Operations Management (COM). COM is the authoritative direction, scheduling, and control of specific collection operations and associated processing, exploitation, and information reporting resources. The COM process specifies how to satisfy the requirement and provides details on how the collection occurs." — AFDP 2-0

> "The CCDR often retains CRM, but as the supported commander for theater ISR, the air component commander leverages the AOC to integrate CRM and COM." — AFDP 2-0

> "The ISRD is the optimal entity for COM of theater airborne ISR operations." — AFDP 2-0

### PIR / RFI / EEI (AFDP 2-0)

> "Priority Intelligence Requirement (PIR). Owned by the commander but developed by the staff. The intelligence component of commander's CCIRs focused the employment of limited intelligence assets and resources against competing demands for intelligence support." — AFDP 2-0

> "To effectively answer these high-level PIRs, the intelligence enterprise breaks them down into more specific, manageable questions called essential elements of information (EEIs)." — AFDP 2-0

> "Request for Information (RFI). Produced by any staff element, these are any specific, time-sensitive, ad hoc requirements for intelligence information or products to support an ongoing crisis or operation not necessarily related to standing requirements or scheduled intelligence production. They are normally the action oriented mechanism for seeking answers to EEIs and other information needs from the intelligence enterprise." — AFDP 2-0

## 3.2 AOC Structure and the ISRD

### Air Operations Center (AOC)

The AOC is the air component commander's (JFACC/CFACC's) primary command and control node for air operations. It contains four primary divisions:

| Division | Role |
|---|---|
| Strategy Division | Campaign planning; long-range targeting; JAOP development |
| Combat Plans Division | Daily master air attack plan (MAAP); ATO construction |
| Combat Operations Division | Real-time execution; dynamic retasking |
| **ISR Division (ISRD)** | **Collection management; intelligence production; targeting support** |

### ISRD Structure and Sub-Elements

> "Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance Division (ISRD). The ISRD, in conjunction with the other AOC divisions, plans and executes airborne ISR operations and provides combat ISR support to air component planning, execution, and assessment activities. The ISRD serves as the central node and fusion center for all air component intelligence operations." — AFDP 2-0

> "Its primary mission is to provide the air component commander an understanding of the adversary and the OE. The ISRD is fundamentally responsible for orienting the commander to enemy capabilities, threats, and COG by directing and managing the air components intelligence operations. It accomplishes this by seamlessly integrating intelligence functions, including analysis, collection management, and targeting support within the air tasking cycle." — AFDP 2-0

**ISRD Sub-Elements:**

| Sub-Element | Doctrinal Title | Function |
|---|---|---|
| ACF Team | Analysis, Correlation, and Fusion Team | All-source analysis; JIPOE/IPB; predictive intelligence |
| ISR Ops Team | ISR Operations and Collection Management | COM of theater airborne ISR; builds CPCL; manages RSTA |
| T/TA Team | Targeting and Tactical Analysis (BDA) | Target development; BDA; restrike recommendations |
| Unit Support | Unit Support Function | Wing-level liaison; RFI process; theater intelligence reporting |

**Analysis (ACF):**
> "Within the ISRD, analysis functions support the joint intelligence preparation of the operational environment (JIPOE), intelligence preparation of the battlespace (IPB), and developing assessments that enhance friendly understanding of the OE and adversary intentions and behaviors. Intelligence analysts synthesize intelligence and data, apply critical thinking, identify, assess, and fill intelligence gaps, and conduct predictive analysis. This is primary coordinated through the Analysis, Correlation, and Fusion Team." — AFDP 2-0

**ISR Operations and Collection Management:**
> "The ISR operations team conducts collection management for theater airborne ISR requirements, planning theater airborne ISR operations, and associated PED. The ISRD is the optimal entity for COM of theater airborne ISR operations. To ensure ISR operations align with air component commander objectives, intelligence operations planners and collection managers attend joint collection working groups and management boards to advocate for air component collection priorities based on PIRs, RFIs, targeting requirements, and other intelligence needs." — AFDP 2-0

**Target Development and BDA:**
> "The ISRD is responsible for target development and battle damage assessment (BDA) primarily done through the targeting and tactical analysis team. Target development includes evaluating and nominating potential adversary targets based on commander's guidance and intended effects. In addition, BDA facilitates reporting on physical and functional damage and change assessments post-strike." — AFDP 2-0

## 3.3 ATO/ACO Production, ISR Annex (RSTA), and SPINS

### Air Tasking Cycle and Collection

> "It accomplishes this by seamlessly integrating intelligence functions, including analysis, collection management, and targeting support within the air tasking cycle." — AFDP 2-0

**AOD (Air Operations Directive):**
> "The AOD is a near-term directive that translates the broader JAOP into daily operational guidance for the air tasking order (ATO). The AOD details the JFACC's intent for a specific ATO or time period, lists PIRs, specifies employment of certain ISR platforms and intelligence disciplines (INTs), and provides PED guidance." — AFDP 2-0

**RSTA (Reconnaissance, Surveillance, and Target Acquisition) Annex:**
> "This guidance is crucial for the daily development of the ISR plan, including the component prioritized collection list (CPCL) and the final reconnaissance, surveillance, and target acquisition (RSTA) Annex. The RSTA serves as the ISR annex to the ATO." — AFDP 2-0

> "Managed by the ISRD, the RSTA synchronizes and directs the employment of all available ISR assets from national systems to theater and tactical platforms to collect information that answers the commanders' PIRs and fills critical intelligence gaps." — AFDP 2-0

> "To build the RSTA, the ISRD coordinates with the theater J2 staff to determine asset availability, evaluates sensors using the AFAT methodology (access, fidelity, accuracy, timeliness) for imagery or signals requirements, and supports targeting and BDA priorities." — AFDP 2-0

**SPINS (Special Instructions):** Part of the ATO package; provides specific guidance for ISR platforms on collection priorities, frequencies, modes, and coordination instructions for a given ATO period.

**CPCL (Component Prioritized Collection List):**
> "The ISRD is responsible for developing the JFACC's CPCL. This list includes collection tied to target nominations and other CRs from various entities within the AOC and subordinate units." — AFDP 2-0

> "These component CPCLs are coordinated with the theater J2 collections team via a JCWG, then consolidated into a draft joint integrated prioritized collection list (JIPCL), which aligns with component target nominations." — AFDP 2-0

## 3.4 Theater ISR Strategy

> "Based on guidance and direction from the CCDR, intelligence (J2) and operations (J3) staffs develop an overall theater collection strategy and posture to execute intelligence operations." — AFDP 2-0

> "The air component ISR strategy is driven by overarching J2 guidance and priorities, and commander PIRs help focus this effort. ISR strategists integrate a wide range of capabilities into the plan: national, theater, and other service platforms, and understand the PED architecture." — AFDP 2-0

> "The ISR operations strategy is a key component of the JAOP, ensuring that collection and analysis are synchronized with theater and national efforts to support the overall campaign." — AFDP 2-0

> "ISR strategists also work with AFFOR staff planners to identify platform, sensor, PED, communications availability, force flow, timing, bed down, and sustainment matters." — AFDP 2-0

## 3.5 Joint Collection Working Group and JCMB (Air Component Perspective)

> "Joint Collection Working Group (JCWG). The JCWG is the primary forum used by a Joint Force Commander's staff to synchronize and prioritize intelligence collection requirements from all subordinate components. The group deconflicts competing requests for limited ISR capabilities and develops the draft joint integrated prioritized collection list (JIPCL) to task these assets effectively." — AFDP 2-0

> "Joint Collection Management Board (JCMB). The JCMB is a key body that oversees and coordinates intelligence collection activities across a joint force, serving as the senior review for the joint collection working group (JCWG). It resolves conflicts in collection priorities, ensures efficient use of ISR assets and formalizes the JIPCL." — AFDP 2-0

> "To exercise these functions, ISR operations planners and collection managers attend joint collection working groups and management boards to advocate for air component collection priorities." — AFDP 2-0

> "From a RAND study: the USAF's AOC doctrine cites that the Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance Division in the AOC should 'monitor and evaluate the ISR strategy for effectiveness in meeting overall ISR requirements, JFC/JFACC PIR, and supporting JFC/JFACC strategy and plans.'" — ASPJ article citing AOC Doctrine (https://www.airuniversity.af.edu/Portals/10/ASPJ/journals/Volume-31_Issue-4/SLP-Haugh_Leonard.pdf)

## 3.6 DCGS-AF (Air Force Distributed Common Ground System)

**System:** AF DCGS (Distributed Common Ground System – Air Force)  
**Operator:** 480th ISR Wing (Langley AFB, VA) and geographically separated units  
**Reference:** GDIT Case Study: https://www.gdit.com/perspectives/case-studies/air-force-dcgs-global-joint-operations/

### Core Concept
AF DCGS is the Air Force's primary ISR processing, exploitation, and dissemination (PED) system and the only intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance system developed to scale for Air Force and Joint operations globally.

> "AF DCGS is a strategic system that supports the Air Force globally. It not only manages sensitive data, but it also allows for the fusion of data from other weapon systems such as high-altitude and medium altitude ISR platforms to support rapid, and in most cases, real-time decision making. Its interoperability with other ISR-networked systems allows the Air Force to fully leverage its data assets to enhance U.S. and allied national security interests." — GDIT

> "The DCGS provides the fused intelligence picture and manages the theater's DOF [disposition of forces], the AOC ISRD transforms that picture into operational direction, tasking, and timing, the IC supplies the global context, strategic warning, and specialized expertise that sharpen every decision. Together, they create a seamless [enterprise]." — AFDP 2-0

> "A critical component of current intelligence is maintaining the adversary's DOF, a product of the DCGS enterprise that provides the precise geographic location and status of known units, directly enabling the Find, Fix, Track (F2T) phase of the dynamic targeting process." — AFDP 2-0

**DCGS Next Gen** is transforming from a PED-focused organization to a "problem-centric, sensor-agnostic, time-dominant, multisource fusion and analysis" model to support JADC2 and GPC. Mission Management Teams (MMTs) are now placed at AOCs worldwide to better integrate DCGS support with AOC collection management.

## 3.7 AFI 14-202 (Intelligence Training and General Rules)

**Publication:** AFI 14-202, Vol. 1 (Intelligence Training), Vol. 2 (Standardization/Evaluation), Vol. 3 (General Intelligence Rules)  
**Reference:** https://irp.fas.org/doddir/usaf/index.html

AFI 14-202 establishes the foundational qualification standards for Air Force intelligence officers, including collection management competencies. Air Force intelligence officers are qualified in four functional competencies:
1. **Analysis**
2. **Collection**
3. **Integration**
4. **Targeting**

> "Knowledge is mandatory of intelligence fundamentals, security, analysis, communications skills, the intelligence disciplines, the intelligence community, ISR information systems, research sources and methods, the presentation of friendly forces, friendly weapons systems, adversary capabilities, ISR operations, targeting, and integration. In addition, officers require depth of knowledge for their assigned functional competency, as identified in AFI 14-202." — DMNA NY Air Guard reference (https://dmna.ny.gov/jobs/air/1500905730-FY%2017-26%20Intelligence%20Officer.pptx)
