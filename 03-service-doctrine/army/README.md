## 2.1 FM 2-0, Intelligence

**Publication:** FM 2-0, Intelligence  
**Current Edition:** October 2023 (updated from 2018)  
**Previous Publicly Available:** https://www.bits.de/NRANEU/others/amd-us-archive/fm2-0fd(09).pdf (2009 version; current 2023 edition at armypubs.army.mil)  
**FAS Index:** https://irp.fas.org/doddir/army/index.html  
**ArmyPubs:** https://armypubs.army.mil/ProductMaps/PubForm/Details.aspx?PUB_ID=1019625

### Core Concept
FM 2-0 is the Army's keystone manual for military intelligence doctrine. It establishes the Intelligence Warfighting Function (IWF) and describes all intelligence tasks, the intelligence process, collection management, and the roles of MI personnel. It forms the foundation for all Army intelligence doctrinal development.

### Intelligence Warfighting Function (IWF)

> "The intelligence warfighting function is the related tasks and systems that facilitate understanding of the operational environment, enemy, terrain, and civil considerations (FM 3-0)." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-30

> "The intelligence warfighting function is one of six warfighting functions—movement and maneuver, intelligence, fires, sustainment, command and control (C2), and protection." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-31

**Four primary intelligence tasks:**
1. Support to Force Generation
2. Support to Situational Understanding
3. Perform ISR
4. Support to Targeting and Information Superiority

### ISR Synchronization vs ISR Integration

> "ISR is an activity that synchronizes and integrates the planning and operation of sensors, assets, and processing, exploitation, and dissemination of systems in direct support (DS) of current and future operations." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-56

> "Intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance synchronization is the task that accomplishes the following: analyzes information requirements and intelligence gaps; evaluates available assets (internal and external); recommends intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance assets; submits requests for information for adjacent and higher collection support; and assesses the effectiveness of the information collection plan." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-57

> "Intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance integration is the task of assigning and controlling a unit's intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance assets (in terms of space, time, and purpose)." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-58

> "ISR planning consists of two significant staff processes: ISR synchronization and ISR integration. ISR synchronization is the responsibility of the intelligence officer and the G-2/S-2 staff. The operations officer is responsible for ISR integration with the support of the intelligence officer." — FM 2-0, Para. 4-13

### The Army Intelligence Process (Five Steps)

> "Intelligence operations are executed by performing five steps that constitute the intelligence process: generate intelligence knowledge, plan, prepare, collect, and produce." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-72

Plus two continuous activities: **assess** and **propagate**.

### Information Collection vs Intelligence (Army-Specific Framing)

Army doctrine deliberately distinguishes "information collection" (the broader operational function managed by the G-3/S-3) from "intelligence" (the product generated by the G-2/S-2):

> "Intelligence is more than just collection. It is a continuous process that involves analyzing information from all sources and conducting operations to develop the situation." — FM 2-0

> "Every Soldier, as a part of a small unit, is a potential information collector and an essential component to help reach situational understanding." — FM 2-0, Para. 1-32

> "Combat information is unevaluated data gathered by or provided directly to the tactical commander which, due to its highly perishable nature or the criticality of the situation, cannot be processed into intelligence in time to satisfy the user's intelligence requirements." — FM 2-0, Para. 4-44

The G-3/S-3 **tasks and directs** collection assets (via orders); the G-2/S-2 **synchronizes and analyzes** ISR and provides recommendations.

### PIR / CCIR / IR Hierarchy (FM 2-0)

**CCIR:**
> "A commander's critical information requirement is an information requirement identified by the commander as being critical to facilitating timely decision making. The two key elements are friendly force information requirements and priority intelligence requirements (JP 3-0)." — FM 2-0, Para. 4-20

**PIR:**
> "A priority intelligence requirement is an intelligence requirement, stated by the commander as a priority for intelligence support which the commander needs to support decision making and to understand the AO or the threat." — FM 2-0, Para. 4-22

**Priority:** "PIRs have first priority in collection assets tasked to their collection." — FM 2-0

### G-2/S-2 Role in Collection

> "It is a commander-driven, coordinated staff effort led by the G-2/S-2." — FM 2-0

> "The G-2/S-2 is not simply a manager but is the primary advisor to the commander on how to utilize ISR assets, supports the commander with analysis and production, and drives ISR collection." — FM 2-0

> "The G-2/S-2 immediately assesses [products] upon receipt for accuracy and relevance." — FM 2-0

> "ISR synchronization is the responsibility of the intelligence officer and the G-2/S-2 staff." — FM 2-0, Para. 4-13

**J-2X / *2X (HUMINT/CI Manager):**
> "The *2X is the CI and HUMINT manager who is authorized to coordinate, deconflict, and synchronize all CI and HUMINT missions in the area of intelligence responsibility." — FM 2-0, Para. 6-25

## 2.2 ATP 2-01, Planning Requirements and Assessing Collection

**Publication:** ATP 2-01, Plan Requirements and Assess Collection  
**Date:** August 2014  
**Public URL:** https://www.bits.de/NRANEU/others/amd-us-archive/atp2_01(14).pdf  
**ArmyPubs:** https://armypubs.army.mil/

### Core Concept
ATP 2-01 is the primary Army collection management techniques publication. It details the collection manager's tasks: developing requirements, building collection planning tools (matrix, overlay, synchronization matrix), and assessing collection effectiveness. It is the "how-to" manual for the G-2/S-2 collection manager.

### Planning Requirements and Assessing Collection Functions

> "The planning requirements and assessing collection functions are— Develop planning requirements. Develop planning requirements tools. Assess information collection. Update planning requirements tools." — ATP 2-01

**Sub-functions of Developing Requirements:**
1. Participate in planning
2. Anticipate requirements
3. Analyze requirements
4. Refine requirements

**Sub-functions of Developing Planning Requirements Tools:**
1. Evaluate resources
2. Develop a collection strategy
3. Submit requests for support (collection)
4. Submit requests for information
5. Match information collection asset capabilities to expected activity
6. Develop SIRs
7. Develop supporting tools

**Sub-functions of Assessing Tactical Task Execution:**
1. Monitor the tactical situation
2. Screen reporting to ensure task completion
3. Correlate reports to requirements
4. Provide feedback to assets
5. Maintain synchronization with operations
6. Cue assets to collection opportunities
7. Recommend retasking of assets

### SIR (Specific Information Requirement) — Army Usage

> "Each requirement is further refined into discrete pieces of information that together answer that requirement. These pieces are referred to as indicators and specific information requirements (SIRs)." — ATP 2-01

> "SIRs help the intelligence staff determine the right combinations of collection assets to provide the timely, specific, and relevant information required." — ATP 2-01

> "SIRs also ensure that information collection taskings correlate with the PIRs and priorities for information collection." — ATP 2-01

**Valid Requirement Criteria:**
> "A valid requirement is necessary, feasible, and complete." — ATP 2-01

**All requirements should specify:** Who (needs results), When (time indicator expected/LTIOV), What (activity or indicator), Where (NAI/TAI), Why (justification), Other (specific instructions).

### Information Collection Matrix (IC Matrix)

> "The information collection matrix links PIRs with indicators, SIRs, NAIs, and TAIs." — ATP 2-01

> "Constructed in a spreadsheet format and including individual work sheets as required, the matrix provides detailed collection and reporting requirements." — ATP 2-01

> "The information collection matrix is not a tasking document." — ATP 2-01

**Matrix columns:**
- Column 1: PIR
- Column 2: Indicators associated with the PIR
- Column 3: SIRs associated with each indicator
- Column 4: NAIs or TAIs associated with each SIR
- Column 5: Starting and ending time for collection
- Remaining columns: Organic resources available for tasking

### Information Collection Overlay (IC Overlay)

> "The operations staff issues an information collection overlay depicting the information collection plan in graphic form as an appendix to annex L (Information Collection) to the operation order." — ATP 2-01

Typical overlay items: friendly boundaries, phase lines, reconnaissance handover lines, NAIs and TAIs, limits of advance, counterreconnaissance areas, UAS flight paths, observation post locations.

### Information Collection Plan (IC Plan)

> "The direct result of the intelligence and operations staffs' efforts is a coordinated information collection plan." — ATP 2-01

> "The information collection plan supports the operation with the necessary information collection assets and the required PED enablers to support collection and decisionmaking." — ATP 2-01

> "The information collection plan positions and tasks collection assets so they can collect the right information, sustain or reconstitute for branches or sequels, or shift priorities as the situation develops." — ATP 2-01

The IC Plan is published as Annex L (Information Collection) to the OPORD.

### Layering: Cueing, Redundancy, Mix

> "Layering ensures the optimal use of limited information collection assets within a unit's task organization." — ATP 2-01
> "Cueing involves the use of one or more information collection assets to provide data that directs collection by other assets." — ATP 2-01
> "Redundancy planning as part of collection strategy development involves the use of several same-discipline (or same-capability) assets to cover the same target." — ATP 2-01
> "Mix means planning for complementary coverage by a combination of assets from multiple intelligence disciplines." — ATP 2-01

### Request for Support (Collection) vs RFI

- **RFI:** Requests existing information from adjacent/higher HQ; answered from on-hand holdings.
- **Request for Support (Collection):** When a gap cannot be answered by available sources, submitted to higher/lateral for incorporation into their IC plan.

## 2.3 ATP 2-01.3, Intelligence Preparation of the Battlefield (IPB)

**Publication:** ATP 2-01.3, Intelligence Preparation of the Battlefield (MCRP 2-10B.1 in joint service edition)  
**Date:** March 2019 (with Change 1, January 2021)  
**Public URL (Marine Corps joint edition):** https://www.marines.mil/portals/1/MCRP%202-10B.1.pdf  
**FAS Entry:** https://irp.fas.org/doddir/army/index.html

### Core Concept
IPB is the continuous, cyclic analytical process that drives collection planning. The four IPB steps produce the intelligence products that identify collection gaps and generate SIRs, NAIs, and event templates that anchor the collection plan.

### Four IPB Steps

| Step | Title | Collection Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Define the Operational Environment | Identifies intelligence gaps → generates initial collection requirements |
| 2 | Describe Environmental Effects on Operations | Determines ISR asset limitations; weather/terrain effects on sensors |
| 3 | Evaluate the Threat/Adversary | Develops threat models, HVT list; generates SIRs focused on capabilities |
| 4 | Determine Threat/Adversary COAs | Produces event template, event matrix, decision support template |

**Step 1 Gap Identification:**
> "Defining the operational environment results in the identification of gaps in current intelligence holdings." — ATP 2-01.3 / MCRP 2-10B.1
> "After determining that information necessary to complete IPB is not contained within local and searchable external data files and databases, staff elements submit requests for information or requests for collection to obtain the information necessary to complete IPB." — ATP 2-01.3

**Collection and PIR linkage:**
> "The IPB process identifies intelligence gaps that are translated into information requirements and priority intelligence requirements (PIRs), which are then answered through collection." — ATP 2-01.3

### NAI and TAI Definitions

> "Named area of interest (NAI): The geographical area where information that will satisfy a specific information requirement can be collected." — (ADRP 1-02, cited in ATP 2-01.3)

> "The areas where the analyst expects key events to occur are designated NAIs." — ATP 2-01.3

**TAI (Target Area of Interest):** Geographic area where high-value targets are expected; a specific sub-type of NAI associated with targeting requirements.

### Indicators

> "An indicator: An item of information which reflects the intention or capability of a threat/adversary to adopt or reject a COA." — ATP 2-01.3 Glossary

> "The activities that reveal the selected COA are called indicators." — ATP 2-01.3

### Event Template and Event Matrix

> "An event template is a graphic overlay used to confirm or deny enemy COAs. The event template depicts the named areas of interest where activity, or its lack of activity, will indicate which course of action the adversary has adopted." — ATP 2-01.3

> "An event matrix is a table that associates the NAI and enemy decision points identified on the event template with indicators to aid in determining which COA the enemy commander is implementing." — ATP 2-01.3

> "The event matrix depicts types of activity expected in each NAI, when the NAI is expected to be active, and any additional information to aid in collection planning." — ATP 2-01.3

### Decision Support Template

> "It ties decision points to NAIs, TAIs, CCIRs, collection assets, and potential friendly response options." — ATP 2-01.3, Appendix A, Para. A-11

## 2.4 FM 3-55, Information Collection

**Publication:** FM 3-55, Information Collection  
**Date:** April 2012 (current update available at armypubs.army.mil)  
**FAS Entry:** https://irp.fas.org/doddir/army/index.html

### Core Concept
FM 3-55 establishes the Army's "information collection" concept as an operations function—broader than intelligence. It defines information collection as a warfighting function task under both intelligence (synchronization) and operations (integration and tasking). This distinguishes Army doctrine from joint doctrine by formally recognizing collection as a cross-functional activity.

### Information Collection (Army Definition)

> "Information collection is an activity that synchronizes and integrates the planning and employment of sensors and assets as well as the processing, exploitation, and dissemination systems in direct support of current and future operations (FM 3-55)." — Cited in ATP 2-01

**Three Information Collection Tasks:**
1. **Plan requirements and assess collection** (G-2/S-2 led)
2. **Task and direct collection** (G-3/S-3 led, with G-2/S-2 recommendation)
3. **Execute collection** (unit/asset execution)

> "Task and direct collection is accomplished through orders and requests. The G-3/S-3 (based on recommendations from the staff) tasks, directs, and, when necessary, retasks the information collection assets." — FM 3-55

### Key Terms

**Information Collection Capability:**
> "An information collection capability as any human or automated sensor, asset, or processing, exploitation, and dissemination (PED) system that can be directed to collect information that enables better decisionmaking, expands understanding of the operational environment, and supports warfighting functions in decisive action." — Cited in ATP 2-01

**Reconnaissance:** "A mission undertaken to obtain, by visual observation or other detection methods, information about activities and resources of an enemy or potential enemy." — FM 2-0 Para. 1-59

**Surveillance:** "The systematic observation of aerospace, surface, or subsurface areas, places, persons, or things by visual, aural, electronic, photographic, or other means." — FM 2-0 Para. 1-60

### Brigade Combat Team (BCT) Intelligence Architecture

The BCT S-2 section performs collection management at the tactical level. Organic collection assets include:
- **SIGINT/EW:** Prophet/Aerial-ISR assets attached/OPCON
- **HUMINT:** MI Company Ground Collection Platoon (one HUMINT OMT + three HCTs, each with MOS 35M)
- **UAS:** Shadow/Gray Eagle for GEOINT/SIGINT/FMV collection
- **GEOINT:** Imagery exploitation capability
- **BCT S-2X:** CI/HUMINT coordination cell

> "Within a BCT there are no organic CI OMTs or CI teams in the MI company. CI support pushed down to the BCT must include an OMT or be controlled by the BCT S-2X/CICA." — FM 2-0, Para. 6-55

BCT collection management uses:
- The IC Synchronization Matrix (draft)
- The IC Matrix (linked to PIRs, Indicators, SIRs, NAIs)
- The IC Overlay (part of OPORD Annex L)
- The planning requirements briefing tool (combined product)

### Military Intelligence Brigade-Theater (MIB-T)

The MIB-T provides the theater army (ASCC) with dedicated intelligence collection, production, and exploitation capability. It controls:
- **All-source intelligence:** Provides AC/WO staff support to ASCC G-2
- **SIGINT:** Provides Army SIGINT capabilities
- **GEOINT:** Provides theater-level imagery exploitation
- **HUMINT:** Provides strategic HUMINT in theater
- **DCGS-A:** Manages Army portion of the DCGS enterprise

MIB-T organic elements support collection management from national-to-tactical integration down to BCT support.

### Roles: 35-Series MOS and 350F WO

| MOS | Title | Collection Role |
|---|---|---|
| 35F | Intelligence Analyst | All-source analysis; supports collection requirements development |
| 35M | HUMINT Collector | Only personnel authorized to conduct HUMINT collection |
| 35N | SIGINT Analyst | Analyzes SIGINT; supports SIGINT collection requirements |
| 35P | CRYPTOLOGIC LNG | SIGINT/HUMINT language support |
| 35L / 351L | Counterintelligence Agent/WO | CI collection operations |
| 350F | All-Source Intelligence WO | Senior collection manager; manages IC plan at BCT/division level |
| 351C | HUMINT Collection Technician (WO) | Manages HUMINT operations; controls HCTs/OMTs |
| 351M | HUMINT Warrant Officer | HUMINT OMT leader at BCT level |

**350F** is the warrant officer MOS for all-source intelligence operations, and the primary collection management WO at division/BCT level. The 350F manages the PIR/collection matrix, advises the G-2/S-2, and interfaces with higher and lateral collection managers.

> "HUMINT collectors are the only personnel authorized to conduct HUMINT collection operations. They are trained and certified enlisted personnel in MOS 35M, warrant officers in MOS 351C and 351M, commissioned officers in MOS 35F…" — FM 2-0, Para. 7-3
