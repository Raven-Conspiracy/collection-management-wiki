## 5.1 MCWP 2-10, Intelligence Operations (MAGTF)

**Publication:** MCWP 2-10, Intelligence Operations  
**Date:** October 2021 (current edition incorporating Force Design 2030)  
**Public URL:** https://www.marines.mil/News/Publications/MCPEL/Electronic-Library-Display/Article/899569/mcwp-2-10/  
**PDF:** https://www.marines.mil/portals/1/Publications/MCWP%202-10.pdf

### Core Concept
> "Marine Corps Warfighting Publication (MCWP) 2-10, Intelligence Operations, builds on the doctrinal foundation established in Marine Corps Doctrinal Publication 2, Intelligence. It provides tactics, techniques, and procedures for Marine air-ground task force intelligence operations. This publication is intended for commanders, intelligence consumers, and personnel who plan and execute intelligence operations." — MCWP 2-10 description, marines.mil

MCWP 2-10 governs intelligence operations for the MAGTF at all echelons (MEF, MEB, MEU). It establishes the intelligence section structure, collection management processes, and the integration of intelligence with MAGTF operations.

### MAGTF Intelligence Architecture

The MAGTF intelligence architecture is built around the G-2/S-2 section, the Intelligence Operations Center (IOC), and the Operations Control and Analysis Center (OCAC):

> "The MEF G-2 owns the intelligence preparation of the battlefield (IPB) but receives inputs, as needed..." — MCA-Marines article on MIGs (https://www.mca-marines.org/wp-content/uploads/30-Intelligence-Within-the-MIGs.pdf)

The G-2 provides overall guidance and direction to the OCAC and IOC. The MEF Intelligence Group (MIG) is the primary collection and IO support element.

### MEF Intelligence Group (MIG)

The MIG plans, coordinates, supports, and conducts operations in the information environment. Within the MIG, key intelligence elements include:
- **Intelligence Battalion** — organic collection; all-source analysis; production
- **Radio Battalion** — SIGINT collection
- **S-2 Alpha (Collection Manager Liaison)** — serves as Collections Manager Liaison to MEF G-2 Collections

> "The MIG intelligence officer provides overall guidance and direction for intelligence operations in the MIG." — MCA-Marines article

> "In this construct, the MEF G-2 owns the intelligence preparation of the battlefield (IPB) but receives inputs from the MIG..." — MCA-Marines article citing MCWP 2-10

### MCWP 2-2 (MAGTF Intelligence Collection — superseded by MCRP framework)

**Note:** MCWP 2-2, MAGTF Intelligence Collection was an older publication. The current framework incorporates collection doctrine within MCWP 2-10 and associated MCRPs.

> "Marine Corps Warfighting Publication (MCWP) 2-2, MAGTF Intelligence Collection, builds on the doctrinal foundation established by Marine Corps Doctrinal Publication (MCDP) 2, Intelligence, and Marine Corps Warfighting Publication (MCWP) 2-1, Intelligence Operations, detailing specific doctrine and tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTP) to conduct intelligence collection to support the Marine air-ground task force (MAGTF). MCWP 2-2 is primarily for intelligence personnel who plan and execute intelligence collection activities." — marines.mil (https://www.marines.mil/News/Publications/MCPEL/Electronic-Library-Display/Article/899570/mctp-2-10a/)

## 5.2 MCWP 2-14 / MCWP 2-6, Counterintelligence

**Publication:** MCWP 2-14, Counterintelligence (redesignated MCWP 2-6, September 2000)  
**Date:** 5 September 2000  
**Public URL:** https://survivalschool.us/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/MCWP-2-6-Counterintelligence.pdf  
**FAS Entry:** https://irp.fas.org/doddir/usmc/index.html

### Core Concept

> "MCWP 2-14, Counterintelligence, complements and expands on this information by detailing doctrine, tactics, techniques, and procedures for the conduct of counterintelligence (CI) operations in support of the Marine air-ground task force (MAGTF). MCWP 2-14 describes aspects of CI operations across the spectrum of MAGTF, naval, joint and multinational operations." — MCWP 2-6 Foreword

### CI Collection Authority (MAGTF)

> "Regardless of the type C2 relationships established, the MAGTF commander will retain technical control (TECHCON) authority over all MAGTF CI and supporting elements, which will be exercised via the G-2/S-2 and intel bn commander/ISC." — MCWP 2-6

> "The unit intelligence officer plans, implements, and supervises the CI effort for the commander." — MCWP 2-6

### CMDO (Collection Management and Dissemination Officer)

> "The CMDO is responsible for formulating detailed intelligence and CI collection requirements (ICRs) and intelligence dissemination requirements (IDR) and tasking and coordinating internal and external operations to satisfy these." — MCWP 2-6

> "The CMDO receives validated PIRs and IRs and direction from the ISC, and then plans and manages the best methods to employ organic and supporting collection and dissemination resources." — MCWP 2-6

Collection management definition echoed in MCWP 2-6:
> "Collection management—The process of converting intelligence requirements into collection requirements, establishing priorities, tasking or coordinating with appropriate collection sources or agencies, monitoring results, and retasking, as required." — MCWP 2-6

### CI Collection Tasks (MAGTF)

> "Conduct tactical CI activities and operations, to include CFSO [CI force protection source operations]." — MCWP 2-6
> "Conduct screening, debriefing, and interrogation of personnel of intelligence/CI interest." — MCWP 2-6
> "Conduct overt HUMINT operations." — MCWP 2-6
> "Conduct CI surveys and evaluations." — MCWP 2-6
> "Conduct TSCM operations." — MCWP 2-6

### CI/HUMINT Integration

> "CI and HUMINT work hand-in-hand because of the nature of their targets and the type of intelligence missions they perform: CI neutralizing the enemy intelligence effort and HUMINT collecting information about enemy activity." — MCWP 2-6

> "The CI/HUMINT Co combines the MEF's CI and IT capabilities into one organization to provide unity of effort of CI and HUMINT operations in support of MAGTF intelligence and force protection needs." — MCWP 2-6

### PI-PIR Linkage

> "Initiate an aggressive CI/HUMINT collection program in response to the MAGTF PIRs and IRs developed by the G-2/S-2 and in support of EEFIs developed by the G-3/S-3." — MCWP 2-6

> "Determine and coordinate the collection effort of PIRs/IRs via organic and supporting CI resources." — MCWP 2-6

## 5.3 Collection Battalions: Radio Battalion, Intel Battalion, Recon Battalion

### Radio Battalion (SIGINT)
The Marine Corps Radio Battalion provides dedicated SIGINT collection support to the MEF. It conducts:
- Electronic signals collection (COMINT, ELINT)
- Direction finding and geolocation
- SIGINT support to targeting and collection management

The Radio Bn is organic to the MEF and is tasked through the G-2 collections process via the ISC/IOC. Requirements are expressed as SIRs tied to PIRs and are submitted through the collection management matrix.

### Intelligence Battalion (All-Source, CI/HUMINT)
The Intel Battalion provides all-source intelligence production, CI/HUMINT collection, and intelligence support across MEF operations. Key roles:
- Manages the ISC (Intelligence Support Cell)
- Operates the IOC (Intelligence Operations Center)
- Executes CI/HUMINT operations via the CI/HUMINT Company
- Provides support to the SARC (Surveillance, Reconnaissance, and Intelligence Group in older doctrine)

> "The intel bn's S-3 section along with the operations center element of the MEF G-2 form the core of the ISC support effort, with planning, direction, and C2 conducted within the IOC's support cell." — MCWP 2-6

> "The CMD section, HQ, Intel Bn, provides the core for MEF CIC collection operations." — MCWP 2-6

### Reconnaissance Battalion (HUMINT/Reconnaissance)
Recon Battalion provides ground reconnaissance and surveillance in support of MAGTF collection plans. Capabilities include:
- Ground reconnaissance (visual, technical)
- Surveillance of NAIs/TAIs
- Clandestine insertion for collection
- HUMINT collection via observer/source operations (coordinated through G-2/S-2)

Recon Bn missions are tasked through the G-3 via the collection plan (Annex B/L to MAGTF OPORD) based on G-2 collection requirements.
