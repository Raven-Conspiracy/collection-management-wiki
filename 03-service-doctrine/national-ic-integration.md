## 6.1 DCGS Family of Systems

The **Distributed Common Ground System (DCGS)** is a family of interoperable ISR processing, exploitation, and dissemination systems fielded across all Services. Each Service operates its own DCGS variant, designed to interoperate through common standards.

| Service | System | Primary URL |
|---|---|---|
| Air Force | AF DCGS (480 ISRW) | https://www.gdit.com/perspectives/case-studies/air-force-dcgs-global-joint-operations/ |
| Army | DCGS-A (Distributed Common Ground System - Army) | https://asc.army.mil/web/portfolio-item/intelligence-electronic-warfare-dcgs-a/ |
| Navy | DCGS-N | https://www.navy.mil/ |
| Marine Corps | DCGS-MC | https://www.marines.mil/ |
| SOCOM | DCGS-SOF | https://www.socom.mil/ |

**Key Concept:**
> "AF DCGS is the only intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) system developed to scale to support Air Force and Joint operations globally. Importantly, it ensures weaponized and secured data gets to the right people at the right time." — GDIT

> "As the Air Force's primary sense makers in the Joint All Domain Command and Control (JADC2) Sensing Grid, the Distributed Common Ground System (DCGS) transformation to the Next Generation (Next Gen) construct postures the DCGS to produce the insight decision-makers and warfighters need to stay one step ahead of our adversaries." — Other Than War Journal (https://othjournal.com/2020/09/26/dcgs-next-generation-accelerating-change-to-deliver-decision-advantage/)

**DCGS-A** provides the Army's theater-level PED capability, managed through the MIB-T and integrated with AF DCGS through common data standards (ICD-specified) and interoperability protocols.

## 6.2 NGA, NSA, NRO: Support to Military Collection

### NGA (National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency)
**Mission:** Production and support of GEOINT  
**GEOINT Functional Manager:** NGA is the IC Functional Manager for GEOINT
- Provides foundational GEOINT products (foundation data, imagery, GEOINT services)
- Supports theater GEOINT cells at CCMDs
- NGA representatives deploy with JIOC and JTF J-2
- Imagery tasking for national systems coordinated through NGA's tasking system

### NSA (National Security Agency)
**Mission:** Production and support of SIGINT; protects US national security systems  
**SIGINT Functional Manager:** NSA is the IC Functional Manager for SIGINT

> "The National Security Agency (NSA) retains CMA over strategic-capable SIGINT ISR systems." — AFDP 2-0 / JP 2-01

> "CCDRs may request, and subsequently receive, temporary SIGINT operational tasking authority (SOTA) over theater-wide capable platforms and sensors. The delegation of SOTA to a CCDR and subsequent delegation of this authority to the JFC ensures the theater can prioritize requirements and focus SIGINT collection where needed to carry out assigned missions." — AFDP 2-0

NSA provides SIGINT operational tasking support through NSA/CSS (Central Security Service) elements at CCMDs.

### NRO (National Reconnaissance Office)
**Mission:** Designs, builds, and operates national reconnaissance systems (satellites and airborne platforms)  
**Collection Asset Ownership:** NRO owns and operates the national technical means (NTM) for imagery and signals collection from space

> "National systems are best employed against high-priority targets outside the range of theater sensors, beyond standoff collection range, and/or in high-threat areas." — JP 2-01

National collection requests for NRO-operated systems flow through the JIOC → DIOCC → DIA/national tasking system → NRO tasking cycle. The NRO does not task itself; tasking goes through the IC collection management system.

### DIA (Defense Intelligence Agency)
**Role:** Defense IC lead for all-source intelligence; manages HUMINT and MASINT functional management  
**HUMINT Functional Manager:** DIA manages the Defense HUMINT Enterprise (15 defense HUMINT executors)  
**MASINT Functional Manager:** DIA manages MASINT requirements and coordination

> "There are 15 defense HUMINT executors. Collectively, they are known as the Defense HUMINT Enterprise." — JP 2-0, Appendix B

DIA's DIOCC (Defense Intelligence Operations Coordination Center) is the primary national-level collection management node for DOD:
> "The DIOCC's primary functions are serving as the DOD lead for collection management and intelligence planning and providing support to combatant command JIOCs and JTFs." — NWP 2-01

## 6.3 Functional Managers and the NIPF

### ICD 113, Functional Managers
**Publication:** Intelligence Community Directive 113, Functional Managers  
**Date:** 19 May 2009  
**Index:** https://irp.fas.org/dni/icd/index.html

ICD 113 establishes the role of IC Functional Managers who oversee collection across each intelligence discipline:

| Functional Manager | Discipline |
|---|---|
| NGA | GEOINT |
| NSA/CSS | SIGINT |
| DIA | HUMINT (Defense) / MASINT |
| CIA/NCS | HUMINT (National Clandestine) |
| USAF | Aerial ISR / GEOINT/SIGINT from airborne platforms |

Functional Managers:
> "Functional Managers shall: Recommend to the DNI national intelligence priorities based on IC-wide customer needs and known information gaps; Ensure that the NIPF guides the performance of their functions, and as appropriate, translate the NIPF into function-specific strategic requirements; Advise the DNI on IC resource allocations to better enable efficient and effective collection against national intelligence priorities." — ICD 204

## 6.4 ICD 204, National Intelligence Priorities Framework (NIPF)

**Publication:** ICD 204, National Intelligence Priorities Framework  
**Current Edition:** 7 January 2021  
**Public URL:** https://www.dni.gov/files/documents/ICD/ICD_204_National_Intelligence_Priorities_Framework_U_FINAL-SIGNED.pdf

### Core Concept
> "The National Intelligence Priorities Framework (NIPF) is the system directed by the President through NSPD-26 for prioritizing national intelligence activities, managing risk, and assessing mission performance. The NIPF provides the mechanism for the development and communication of overarching national intelligence priorities and the evaluation of IC responsiveness and progress against those priorities." — ICD 204

### Collection Requirements and NIPF

> "IC collection requirements and programs of analysis shall reflect national intelligence priorities by using common NIPF codes." — ICD 204

> "National intelligence priorities, and subsequent evaluations of IC responsiveness to those priorities, shall inform the allocation of resources to IC collection and analytic postures, as well as National Intelligence Program (NIP) planning, programming, budgeting, and evaluation activities." — ICD 204

### Military Collection and NIPF

> "[The DNI will] determine requirements and priorities for, and manage and direct the tasking of national intelligence by elements of the IC, including approving requirements for collection and analysis and resolving conflicts in collection requirements and in the tasking of national collection assets of IC elements (except when otherwise directed by the President or when the Secretary of Defense exercises collection tasking authority under plans and arrangements approved by the Secretary of Defense and the DNI)." — ICD 204

**Practical implication for collection managers:** PIR-driven collection requirements submitted by CCMDs to national agencies are prioritized using NIPF codes. CCMD collection managers must understand how their PIRs map to NIPF priorities when competing for national collection assets.

### Roles

- **DNI:** Approves national intelligence priorities; may change only by Presidential direction or emerging needs
- **DDMI (Deputy Director for Mission Integration):** DNI's designee for NIPF development, management, and oversight
- **Intelligence Topic Experts (ITEs):** Senior SMEs assigned to each NIPF topic; adjudicate priorities
- **Functional Managers:** Translate NIPF into function-specific strategic requirements for their disciplines
- **NIMs (National Intelligence Managers):** Develop priorities within their mission areas
- **IC Elements:** Align collection and analytic resources to NIPF priorities

## 6.5 ICD 300, Management, Integration, and Oversight of Intelligence Collection

**Publication:** ICD 300, Management, Integration, and Oversight of Intelligence Collection and Covert Action  
**Date:** 3 October 2006  
**Index:** https://irp.fas.org/dni/icd/index.html

ICD 300 establishes the DNI's authority for IC-wide collection management and oversight. It governs:
- IC-wide requirements for intelligence collection
- Coordination mechanisms between IC elements and national collection managers
- Collection management policies and standards across the IC

This ICD, combined with ICD 204 and the functional manager authorities under ICD 113, forms the top-level framework within which all national-level collection management operates.

**Related ICD 302:** Document and Media Exploitation (DOMEX) — governs intelligence collection from captured documents and media.

**ICD 304:** Human Intelligence — governs HUMINT collection across the IC.

## 6.6 PIR/SIR at the National Level

At the national level, the NIPF establishes top-level intelligence priorities, which are then translated into collection requirements by national collection managers using a process analogous to (but distinct from) the PIR/SIR hierarchy used in military operations:

| Level | Mechanism | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| National Strategic | NIPF topics and priorities | DNI / NSC / President |
| National Collection | National Collection Requirements | DNI / Functional Managers / IC Elements |
| Theater | PIR / JIPCL | CCMD J-2 / JIOC |
| Operational | PIR / Component Collection List | JTF J-2 / AOC ISRD |
| Tactical | PIR / SIR / IC Matrix | BCT/Battalion S-2 |

National-level SIRs (also sometimes called EEIs at the national level) feed into collection databases like COLISEUM, which tracks requirements from the national down to the tactical level.

## 6.7 Library of National Intelligence (LNI)

> "The Library of National Intelligence (LNI) shall serve as the authoritative repository of IC disseminated intelligence collection reporting, analytic production, and other DNI-designated mission performance information for the purposes of tracking and evaluating IC responsiveness to national intelligence priorities." — ICD 204
