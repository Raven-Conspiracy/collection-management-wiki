## 1.5 Collection Management Cycle: Requirements, Mission, and Asset Management

### The Three Functions of Collection Management (JP 2-01 framework)

| Function | Authority | What It Governs |
|---|---|---|
| Collection Requirements Management (CRM) | CMA holder (CCMD J-2) | Validates what must be collected; maintains JIPCL |
| Collection Operations Management (COM) | Delegated to component/asset controller | How and when to collect; tasks specific sensors |
| Collection Management Authority (CMA) | CCMD J-2 (may delegate to JTF) | Overall authority to prioritize, validate, task |

### Requirements Management Process Flow
1. Commander approves PIRs (CCIR development)
2. J-2 staff develops EEIs and SIRs under each PIR
3. Collection requirements matrix (CRMx) is built
4. Requirements validated — "necessary, feasible, and complete"
5. Collection strategy developed: disciplines identified, NAIs/TAIs established
6. JIPCL populated and prioritized
7. Forwarded to JCWG → JCMB for approval

### Asset Management (COM Functions)
- Match SIRs/EEIs to capable collection assets
- Evaluate assets against: capability, availability, survivability, LTIOV
- Apply cueing, redundancy, and mix
- Issue collection tasking with PED guidance
- Monitor execution via ISR visualization tools
- Retask based on assessment/feedback

## 1.6 ISR Synchronization Matrix and ISR Sync Tool

The **ISR Synchronization Matrix** (called "Integrated Collection Planning Matrix" in JP 2-01; "Information Collection Synchronization Matrix" in Army doctrine) is the primary collection planning tool. It links:

| Column | Content |
|---|---|
| PIR | The commander's priority intelligence question |
| EEI | Critical sub-questions answering the PIR |
| Indicator | Observable activity confirming/denying threat COA |
| SIR | Specific data point needed to assess the indicator |
| NAI/TAI | Geographic or nodal location for collection |
| LTIOV | Deadline for the information to have value |
| Asset/Resource | Specific collection platform or discipline tasked |
| Collection Discipline | HUMINT/SIGINT/GEOINT/MASINT/OSINT |

The **ISR Sync Tool** (in DCGS and related systems) provides a digital, real-time management interface for collection managers to see asset status, task assignments, mission windows, and collection gaps.

## 1.7 National-to-Tactical Integration (NTI)

### Information Architecture

| Network/System | Function |
|---|---|
| JWICS | SCI data/VTC connecting deployed forces to national producers |
| JDISS | Access to theater, Service, and national intelligence resources |
| IBS (Integrated Broadcast Service) | Near-real-time intelligence to the warfighter |
| GCCS-I3 | Imagery and intelligence for commanders and staffs |
| Intelink | Principal electronic means for intelligence product dissemination |

> "The 'pull' concept involves direct electronic access to databases, intelligence files, or other repositories by intelligence organizations at all levels." — JP 2-01, pp. III-66 to III-67

> "DIA ensures the expeditious flow of intelligence from the national level through the JIOCs to deployed forces during peacetime." — JP 2-01, p. III-11

> "Requests for national agency support are normally forwarded to DIA using COLISEUM." — JP 2-01, p. II-24

**COLISEUM** (Community On-Line Intelligence System for End-users and Managers) is the DOD standard system for submitting and tracking RFIs from theater JIOCs to national agencies.

**DIOCC** (Defense Intelligence Operations Coordination Center): "The DIOCC's primary functions are serving as the DOD lead for collection management and intelligence planning and providing support to combatant command JIOCs and JTFs." — NWP 2-01
