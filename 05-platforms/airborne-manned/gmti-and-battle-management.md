## 8. GMTI / Battle Management — Airborne Manned Platforms

### 8.1 E-8C Joint STARS (LEGACY — Retired 2023)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | E-8C Joint Surveillance Target Attack Radar System (JSTARS) |
| **Operator** | USAF / ANG (116th Air Control Wing, Robins AFB GA) — **Retired March 2023** |
| **Type** | Manned Battle Management / C2ISR / GMTI Aircraft (Legacy) |
| **Collection Capability** | AN/APY-7 side-looking phased-array radar in 27-ft canoe radome: SAR imagery of large areas + Wide-Area MTI (WAMTI) for ground vehicle tracking; could simultaneously track hundreds of moving and stationary targets; 17+ operator consoles for battle management |
| **Performance Envelope** | Boeing 707-320 airframe; Ceiling: 42,000 ft; Endurance: 9–11 hours (aerial refueling capable); Crew: 3 flight + 18–34 mission crew; Standoff range: 250 km |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | Joint STARS Ground Station Module (GSM) JTIDS/Link-16; HF/VHF/UHF voice; SATCOM; interoperable with ground C2 nodes |
| **PED Architecture** | Onboard real-time battle management; Ground Station Modules (GSM); Army/AF exploitation cells |
| **Historical Note** | Combat debut Operation Desert Storm 1991; continuous use through OIF/OEF; no direct replacement; capability gap acknowledged upon retirement |
| **Public Sources** | [AF Fact Sheet JSTARS](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104507/e-8c-joint-stars/) | [The War Zone JSTARS retirement](https://www.twz.com/e-8-jstars-has-flown-its-last-operational-mission) |

### 8.2 E-3G Sentry (AWACS) — AIR PICTURE

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | E-3G Sentry (modernized; formerly E-3B/C) |
| **Operator** | USAF (552nd Air Control Wing, Tinker AFB OK; NATO E-3A force at Geilenkirchen; Saudi Arabia; Chile; France) |
| **Type** | Airborne Warning and Control System (AWACS) / Air Battle Management |
| **Collection Capability** | AN/APY-2 rotating radar in 30-ft diameter rotodome (6 rpm): simultaneous air and maritime surveillance to 320+ km range; IFF; all-altitude, all-weather; 360° coverage; identifies and tracks 600+ aircraft simultaneously; TADIL/Link-16 integration; passive ESM sensors; passive threat warning; air picture to ground/surface forces |
| **Sensor Names** | AN/APY-2 pulse-Doppler radar (RSIP upgrade); IFF interrogator; ESM suite |
| **Performance Envelope** | Boeing 707-320 airframe; Ceiling: 29,000 ft; Endurance: 8 hours (14+ with refueling); Max speed: 530 mph; Range: 5,000 nm; Crew: 4 flight + 13–19 mission specialists |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | TADIL (Link-16); HF/VHF/UHF; SATCOM; Joint Tactical Information Distribution System (JTIDS) |
| **PED Architecture** | Onboard real-time battle management; Joint Air Operations Center (JAOC)/CAOC connectivity; NATO/allied integration |
| **Tasking Authority** | JFACC/CFACC; CAOC; NATO CAOC |
| **Primary Mission Set** | Airspace battle management; air picture compilation; C2 of air assets; early warning; battle management for offensive and defensive counter-air; air-to-air tasking |
| **Limitations** | Aging Boeing 707 airframe; structural fatigue concerns; maintenance intensive; being replaced by E-7A Wedgetail (though USAF has reconsidered purchase in 2025, favoring Golden Dome and E-2D) |
| **Program Status** | Active but being retired; E-7A replacement program under review as of 2025 |
| **Public Sources** | [AF Fact Sheet AWACS](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104504/e-3-sentry-awacs/) | [Wikipedia E-3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boeing_E-3_Sentry) |

### 8.3 E-7A Wedgetail (USAF AEW&C Procurement — Status in Flux)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | E-7A Wedgetail (Boeing 737-700/AEW&C) |
| **Operator** | RAAF (6 aircraft, primary operator); USAF (procurement in EMD as of 2024; status under review 2025); RAF (5 ordered); South Korea; Turkey |
| **Type** | Airborne Early Warning and Control (AEW&C) |
| **Collection Capability** | Northrop Grumman MESA (Multi-Role Electronically Scanned Array) fixed AESA radar: simultaneous air-to-air and air-to-surface surveillance; 360° coverage via fixed arrays (no rotating dome); emitter detection/geolocation (ESM); 10 mission crew consoles |
| **Sensor Names** | MESA AESA radar (S-band active); ESM suite; IFF |
| **Performance Envelope** | Boeing 737-700 platform; twin CFM56 engines; Endurance: ~10+ hours; Ceiling: 41,000 ft; Speed: ~486 mph; Half the crew requirement of E-3 |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | Link-16; SATCOM; NATO-compatible |
| **Program Status** | USAF EMD contract awarded early 2024; USAF DoD announced intention to cancel USAF E-7 purchase June 2025 in favor of space-based (Golden Dome) and E-2D Advanced Hawkeye solutions; RAAF fully operational |
| **Public Sources** | [Wikipedia E-7](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boeing_E-7_Wedgetail) | [Air and Space Forces E-7 article](https://www.airandspaceforces.com/air-force-to-buy-more-e-7s-contract-modifications/) |
