## 12. Cyber / National SIGINT Collection

### 12.1 NSA/CSS National SIGINT Collection Architecture

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Organization** | National Security Agency / Central Security Service (NSA/CSS), Fort Meade, MD |
| **Mission** | Global monitoring, collection, and processing of signals intelligence (SIGINT); protection of US communications |
| **Collection Enterprise** | SIGINT collection encompasses COMINT (communications) and ELINT (electronic); managed through a global architecture of collection platforms, sensors, and processing nodes |
| **NSA Collection Sites** | NSA Headquarters (Ft. Meade MD); NSA Colorado (Buckley SFB — overhead SIGINT technical collection); NSA Georgia (Gordon); NSA Hawaii (Pearl Harbor); NSA Texas (Lackland AFB); plus overseas sites and FVEY (Five Eyes) partner collection |
| **Cryptologic Service Groups (CSGs)** | NSA/CSS Cryptologic Service Groups (CSGs) embedded with combatant commands provide direct SIGINT support to theater commanders |
| **Collection Disciplines** | COMINT (communications intelligence); ELINT (electronic intelligence); FISINT (foreign instrumentation signals intelligence — telemetry); MASINT-related overhead SIGINT; cyber SIGINT (CNE — computer network exploitation) |
| **Oversight** | Executive Order 12333; Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA); PPD-28; SSCI/HPSCI oversight |
| **Public Sources** | [NSA Locations](https://www.nsa.gov/about/locations/) | [Wikipedia NSA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Security_Agency) |

### 12.2 Cyber Mission Force (CMF) — DNINT / Collection Role

Per **JP 3-12 (Cyberspace Operations)** and DoD Cyber Strategy:

| Element | Collection Role |
|---|---|
| **Cyber National Mission Forces (CNMF)** | Defend US critical infrastructure; in collection context, conduct CNE (Computer Network Exploitation) to gather intelligence on adversary networks at national priority level |
| **Cyber Combat Mission Forces (CCMF)** | Support CCMD operations; CNE in support of CCMD intelligence requirements; offensive cyber enabled by intelligence collection |
| **Cyber Protection Forces (CPF)** | Primarily defensive; passive collection of adversary TTPs during defense contributes to cyber threat intelligence |
| **Army Cyber Command (ARCYBER)** | Electronic warfare and cyber integrated operations; tactical cyber collection at echelon; EW/SIGINT/cyber convergence |
| **CYBERCOM Intelligence Support** | Served by NSA/CSS via dual-hat NSA Director = CYBERCOM Commander relationship; NSA provides SIGINT in support of cyber operations |

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Authority Document** | JP 3-12 (Cyberspace Operations, 2018); DoD Cyber Strategy; NSPM-13 (classified) |
| **Tasking Authority** | CYBERCOM; theater CCMD J2; NSA for national-level cyber intelligence |
| **PED Architecture** | NSA/CSS; CYBERCOM intelligence; DCGS integration for cyber-derived intelligence products |
| **Collection Manager Note** | Cyber collection (DNI/DNINT) requires specific Title 10/Title 50 authorities; CNE collection requirements processed through similar collection management framework as SIGINT |
| **Public Sources** | [JP 3-12 via Army War College](https://csl.armywarcollege.edu/pubs/Publications/Strategic_Cyberspace_Operations_Primer-2023_Dec_18.pdf) | [Army Cyber Defense Review](https://cyberdefensereview.army.mil/CDR-Content/Articles/Article-View/Article/1136012/notes-on-military-doctrine-for-cyberspace-operations-in-the-united-states-1992/) |
