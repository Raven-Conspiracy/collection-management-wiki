## 7. GEOINT / IMINT / SIGINT — Space-Based Platforms

### 7.1 KH-11 Kennen / Crystal (NRO Electro-Optical Imaging)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | KH-11 series (Kennen/Crystal designation series; Block I through current Block 8+); USA-number designations |
| **Operator** | NRO (National Reconnaissance Office); NGA exploitation |
| **Type** | National Technical Means (NTM) — Electro-Optical Imaging Reconnaissance Satellite |
| **Collection Capability** | Digital EO imaging in multiple spectral bands (VNIR, SWIR); near-real-time downlink via ground stations; some ELINT collection reportedly co-hosted; mirror diameter publicly known ~2.4 m (equivalent to Hubble Space Telescope); theoretical ground resolution ~15 cm (atmospheric effects reduce operational resolution) |
| **Performance Envelope** | Low-Earth orbit (LEO), ~250–1,000 km altitude; polar/sun-synchronous orbit; revisit rate dependent on constellation size and orbit; near-real-time downlink via TDRSS and dedicated ground stations |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | Near-real-time EO downlink via relay satellites; TDRSS |
| **PED Architecture** | NGA (National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency) — primary exploitation and dissemination; NSA for ELINT payloads; IC-wide dissemination via classified networks |
| **Tasking Authority** | National: DNI/NRO collection management; served by IC requirement collectors; CCMD J2 via JCS collection requirements |
| **Primary Mission Set** | Strategic optical IMINT; order-of-battle assessment; treaty monitoring; facility monitoring; indications & warnings; target development for national and theater consumers |
| **Limitations** | Weather/cloud cover limits optical collection (unlike SAR); revisit rate limited by orbital mechanics and constellation size; collection focus requires national-level prioritization; contested space environment risk |
| **Public Sources** | [NRO Kennen Declassification 2021](https://www.nro.gov/Portals/135/documents/news/press/2021/2021-06-60th%20Anniversary%20Declassification_11162021.pdf) | [Wikipedia KH-11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KH-11_KENNEN) | [Spaceflight101/Skyrocket](https://space.skyrocket.de/doc_sdat/kh-11.htm) |

### 7.2 Lacrosse / Onyx (NRO SAR Imaging Constellation)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | Lacrosse (original designation); Onyx (later designation post-1998); 5 satellites launched 1988–2005 |
| **Operator** | NRO; NGA exploitation |
| **Type** | National Technical Means (NTM) — Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Imaging Satellite |
| **Collection Capability** | SAR imaging capable of cloud/weather penetration; soil/foliage limited penetration; resolution estimated ~1 meter; active radar illumination enables day/night, all-weather collection; area vs spotlight modes |
| **Performance Envelope** | LEO orbit (~400–700 km); Lacrosse 1 launched 1988; Lacrosse 5 launched 2005; later satellites likely replaced by classified successors |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | TDRSS relay; classified ground stations |
| **PED Architecture** | NGA; NSA; DIA for technical intelligence |
| **Tasking Authority** | NRO collection management; national-level tasking |
| **Primary Mission Set** | All-weather SAR IMINT; complement to KH-11 optical in cloud-cover scenarios; GMTI-capable versions for moving target detection; treaty monitoring; facility assessment |
| **Limitations** | Active radar illumination provides minimal self-protection; revisit rate orbital limitation; existence of Onyx satellites formally declassified by NRO in July 2008 |
| **Public Sources** | [Wikipedia Lacrosse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacrosse_(satellite)) | [Skyrocket Onyx](https://space.skyrocket.de/doc_sdat/onyx.htm) | [NRO SAR 2008 declassification](https://www.eoportal.org/satellite-missions/nro-radar-data) |

### 7.3 SBIRS (Space-Based Infrared System) — MASINT / Missile Warning

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | SBIRS GEO-1 through GEO-6; SBIRS HEO-1 through HEO-4 (hosted payloads on classified satellites) |
| **Operator** | USSF (Space Delta 4 — missile warning); operated from Buckley SFB CO (primary) and Schriever SFB CO (backup); Air National Guard for some operations |
| **Type** | Geosynchronous and Highly Elliptical Orbit Infrared MASINT/Missile Warning Satellite Constellation |
| **Collection Capability** | Dual-band IR sensors (scanning and staring modes); simultaneous widearea scanning and focused staring over areas of interest; SBIRS GEO uses scanning IR for global surveillance and staring IR for in-theater focused collection; HEO payloads provide superior northern-hemisphere coverage of Russian/Chinese launch sites; 4 mission areas: Missile Warning (MW), Missile Defense (MD), Technical Intelligence (TI/MASINT), Battlespace Characterization (BSC) |
| **Sensor Names** | Scanning IR sensor; Staring IR sensor (GEO); hosted HEO IR payloads |
| **Performance Envelope** | 4 GEO satellites in geosynchronous orbit (~22,236 miles); 4 HEO payloads in ~63° highly elliptical orbits; complements legacy DSP (Defense Support Program) constellation; 12 total SBIRS/STSS payloads launched |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | S-band and X-band control links; classified data downlinks to ground processing |
| **PED Architecture** | USSF Space Delta 4; NORAD/NORTHCOM for missile warning; MDA for missile defense cueing; DIA for TECHINT; real-time integration to missile defense command and control |
| **Tasking Authority** | USSF/SPACECOM; SECDEF for strategic warning; NORAD/NORTHCOM for tactical missile defense cueing |
| **Primary Mission Set** | Strategic and theater ballistic missile launch detection; ICBM/SLBM warning; SRBM/MRBM tactical warning; weapons effects characterization; battlespace characterization (e.g., nuclear detonation detection) |
| **Limitations** | GEO orbit limits revisit rate to fixed sectors; complemented by HEO for polar gaps; adversary ASAT threats to GEO constellation; successors (NGG — Next Generation OPIR/Geosynchronous) being developed |
| **Program Status** | Full constellation operational (GEO-6 launched Feb 2022); transition to NGG (Next Gen OPIR) constellation in progress |
| **Public Sources** | [USSF Fact Sheet](https://www.spaceforce.mil/about-us/fact-sheets/article/2197746/space-based-infrared-system/) | [Lockheed Martin SBIRS](https://www.lockheedmartin.com/en-us/products/sbirs.html) | [Wikipedia SBIRS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space-Based_Infrared_System) |

### 7.4 NRO Commercial Imagery Integration (EOCL / CSO Programs)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Program** | EOCL (Electro-Optical Commercial Layer) — NRO commercial satellite imagery contracts; Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) for broader vendor access |
| **Primary Operators / Vendors** | **Maxar** (WorldView-1/2/3/4; GeoEye-1 — 30 cm resolution optical); **Planet** (PlanetScope, SkySat — daily revisit, 0.5m SkySat); **BlackSky** (Gen-2 — sub-1m, high-revisit monitoring); **Capella Space** (SAR — 0.5m spotlight, all-weather); **Umbra** (SAR — 0.25m, highest-resolution commercial SAR) |
| **Contract Vehicle** | EOCL 10-year contracts effective May 2022 (Maxar, BlackSky, Planet) — billions of dollars; Capella NRO contract since Dec 2019; CSO for new/emerging vendors |
| **Collection Capability** | Optical EO (WorldView-3: 31 cm panchromatic, 1.24 m multi-spectral, short-wave IR); Radar SAR (Capella/Umbra: cloud-penetrating, day/night, 0.25–1m); daily revisit constellations for change detection; AIS integration for maritime vessels |
| **PED Architecture** | NGA exploitation; IC-wide via classified cloud; ~500,000+ IC/DoD users; API-enabled commercial imagery ordering architecture |
| **Tasking Authority** | NGA commercial acquisition; CCMD requests through NGA Commercial Imagery Program (CIP); JFACC for theater requirements |
| **Primary Mission Set** | Gap-filling for NTM (national technical means) coverage; change detection; battlespace preparation; OSINT-quality assessments; commercial SAR for all-weather support |
| **Limitations** | Unclassified imagery (some contracts); subject to commercial licensing restrictions; adversary spoofing/denial of commercial imagery possible; resolution gap vs KH-11 national means |
| **Public Sources** | [NRO EOCL Press Release 2022](https://www.nro.gov/Portals/65/documents/news/press/2022/press_release_05-22.pdf) | [Satellite Today EOCL](https://www.satellitetoday.com/government-military/2022/05/25/nro-awards-imagery-contracts-to-maxar-blacksky-and-planet-worth-billions/) | [BlackSky Extension](https://blacksky.com/press-releases/blacksky-awarded-electro-optical-commercial-layer-contract-extension-from-national-reconnaissance-office/) |

### 7.5 USSF Silent Barker (Space Situational Awareness)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Designation** | Silent Barker (also Silentbarker); NROL-107 payload; joint NRO/USSF mission |
| **Operator** | USSF (Space Delta 2 — space domain awareness); NRO operational management |
| **Type** | Geosynchronous Space Situational Awareness (SSA) / Space Domain Awareness (SDA) Satellite Constellation |
| **Collection Capability** | Space-based EO/IR sensors for detection and tracking of space objects in geosynchronous orbit; designed for indications and warnings (I&W) of threats against high-value GEO assets; "watchdog" for the GEO regime; supports custody of adversary satellites and debris |
| **Performance Envelope** | GEO orbit; multiple payloads; constellation approach (at least several satellites); launched Sep 2023 (initial) |
| **Datalinks / Comms** | Classified downlinks; integration with Space Fence and Space Surveillance Network |
| **PED Architecture** | USSF Space Delta 2; SPACECOM; IC space intelligence element |
| **Tasking Authority** | USSF/SPACECOM; NRO |
| **Primary Mission Set** | On-orbit threat characterization; adversary satellite tracking in GEO; I&W for rendezvous/proximity operations; complement to ground-based Space Surveillance Network |
| **Follow-On** | USSF soliciting small, low-cost commercial SSA constellation as Silent Barker successor |
| **Public Sources** | [NRO NROL-107 Press Kit](https://www.nro.gov/Portals/135/Documents/news/Press%20Kits/10240_PressKitbook_Launch_NROL-107.pdf) | [Defense Scoop Silent Barker launch](https://defensescoop.com/2023/09/10/silentbarker-launch-nro-space-force/) |
