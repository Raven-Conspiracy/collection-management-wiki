## 1. Doctrinal Definition

Measurement and Signature Intelligence (MASINT) occupies a distinct and technically demanding position within the U.S. Intelligence Community's collection taxonomy. The authoritative joint-doctrine definition, codified in **JP 2-0, Appendix B**, reads:

> *"MASINT is information produced by quantitative and qualitative analysis of physical attributes of targets and events to characterize, locate, and identify them. MASINT exploits a variety of phenomenologies to support signature development and analysis, to perform technical analysis, and to detect, characterize, locate, and identify targets and events. MASINT is derived from specialized, technically-derived measurements of physical phenomenon intrinsic to an object or event and it includes the use of quantitative signatures to interpret the data."*
> — [JP 2-0, Joint Intelligence, Appendix B (2013)](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf)

The same appendix elaborates the **measurement** element as referring to "actual measurements of parameters of an event or object such as the demonstrated flight profile and range of a cruise missile," while **signatures** are "typically the products of multiple measurements collected over time and under varying circumstances" that are "used to develop target classification profiles and discrimination and reporting algorithms for operational surveillance and weapon systems." ([JP 2-0, Appendix B](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf))

The [ODNI MASINT Primer (2022)](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf) translates this into operational language: "If the target *vibrates, makes a sound, leaves a trace, or gets hot or cold*, it could have an exploitable signature. MASINT measures the way things are and how they actually perform." A **signature** is formally defined by the ODNI as "a unique set of characteristics that differentiate one item or event from another."

The [IC21 House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence report (1996)](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html) provides an earlier but still widely cited formulation: MASINT is "technically derived intelligence (excluding traditional imagery and signal intelligence) which when collected, processed, and analyzed, results in intelligence that detects, tracks, identifies, or describes the signatures (distinctive characteristics) of fixed or dynamic target sources." That same report popularized the analogy: where SIGINT is akin to sound and IMINT to sight, "MASINT is akin to touch, taste and smell."

The [AFDP 2-0, Intelligence (2026)](https://www.doctrine.af.mil/Portals/61/documents/AFDP_2-0/2-0-AFDP-INTELLIGENCE.pdf) concisely states that MASINT "collects and produces precise threat characteristics and performance information essential to support planning, development, and application of weapon systems, countermeasures, targeting, and battle damage assessment" and is "especially useful to monitor adversary technical developments and deployments, as well as emerging threats from weapons of mass destruction."

MASINT was formally recognized as an intelligence discipline in 1986, when the IC created the MASINT Committee to oversee all MASINT activities. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html)) Because of the highly technical and scientific means used to develop signatures, MASINT became known within the IC as the *"INT of science."* ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

Uniquely among collection disciplines, MASINT straddles disciplinary boundaries: it is "both a true, unique collection/analysis discipline and a highly refined analytical technique of the traditional disciplines" of IMINT and SIGINT. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html)) The ODNI makes the distinction explicit: the same Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) energy processed through GEOINT methods produces imagery, while the same energy processed through MASINT methods produces signatures — the outcome differs even when the source energy is identical. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

## 2. Functional Manager (ICD 113)

**Intelligence Community Directive 113 (ICD 113), Functional Managers**, effective 19 May 2009, designates the Director of the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA) as both the **IC MASINT Functional Manager** and the **DoD MASINT Manager**. The [ODNI Intelligence Community page](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/who-we-are/leadership/deputy-dni-for-policy-capabilities/121-dni/intelligence-community) describes this dual role explicitly: "The Director of DIA is both the 'Intelligence Community Functional Manager for MASINT' and the 'DOD MASINT Manager.' The National MASINT Office (NMO) manages and executes MASINT services of common concern and related activities for the D/DIA in response to National and Department of Defense requirements."

**Historical evolution of the MASINT functional manager:**

The lineage of DIA's MASINT management responsibility traces to 1992–1993. Following the Gulf War, the Secretary of Defense issued a memorandum on 25 August 1992 assigning DIA responsibility for central management of MASINT (DoD Directive 5105.21, as amended). This led directly to Director of Central Intelligence Directive (DCID) 2/11, "Management of National Measurement and Signature Intelligence," signed 6 April 1993, which placed formal national-level MASINT management with the DIA. ([FAS/IRP Evaluation Report on MASINT](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

The **Central MASINT Office (CMO)** was formally established within DIA in 1993, becoming "the designated collection manager of MASINT" and the single point of contact for all MASINT policy, programs, and requirements. ([FAS/IRP Overview of the IC](https://irp.fas.org/offdocs/int023.html)) The CMO consisted of four operational divisions: Plans, Policy, and Requirements (CMO-1); Functional Management (CMO-2); Central MASINT Operations Center (CMO-3); and Technology (CMO-4), plus the MASINT Committee Staff and a Technology Coordination Office detachment. ([FAS/IRP Evaluation Report on MASINT](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

By the late 1990s, a DoD Inspector General evaluation found the CMO non-operational in 16 of 18 assigned responsibilities — primarily due to resource shortfalls and organizational placement within DIA's Directorate for Intelligence Operations rather than directly under the Director, DIA. ([FAS/IRP Evaluation Report on MASINT](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm)) Congressional pressure through the IC21 report and subsequent directives drove reforms.

Following enactment of the Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of 2004 and issuance of ICD 113, the CMO was redesignated as the **National MASINT Management Office (NMMO)** — a joint DIA/IC organization — and the role of functional manager was formally codified under DNI authority. A 2009 Acting PDNI Memorandum (Establishment of a Functional Manager for MASINT, 17 July 2009) reaffirmed ICD 113's designation. ([DIA/NMMO Charter, via MASCOM Charter document](https://www.dia.mil/FOIA/FOIA-Electronic-Reading-Room/FileId/237608/)) The **National MASINT Committee (MASCOM)** was formally chartered to assist and advise the MASINT Functional Manager and the DNI across the IC. ([DIA MASCOM Charter](https://www.dia.mil/FOIA/FOIA-Electronic-Reading-Room/FileId/237608/))

**[DoDI 5105.58 (2009)](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)** formalizes the Director, DIA's responsibilities as DoD MASINT Manager: advising the Secretary of Defense, USD(I&S), Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, and Combatant Commanders on MASINT issues; establishing, leading, and operating a unified defense intelligence community for MASINT; managing the NMMO and the U.S. MASINT System (USMS); and submitting nominations for MASCOM Chair to the DNI for approval.

## 3. Collection Authorities and Legal Anchors

The legal and policy framework for MASINT collection spans national intelligence law, executive orders, and DoD directives. Key instruments include:

**Executive Order 12333 (as amended)** governs all U.S. intelligence activities including MASINT, establishing the overall authority under which DoD MASINT collection operations are conducted. [DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf) explicitly cites E.O. 12333 as the basis for USD(I&S) fiscal management authority over DoD MASINT within the Military Intelligence Program (MIP).

**DoDI 5105.58 (April 22, 2009, as amended through Change 2, September 24, 2020)** is the primary DoD authority establishing MASINT policy, assigning responsibilities, and providing management instructions across all DoD Components. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) It makes clear that DoD MASINT activities must be executed in a "coordinated, coherent, and decentralized manner" in support of national security objectives.

**DIA as Combat Support Agency — DIA authority** derives from DoD Directive 5105.21, which designates DIA as a DoD combat support agency. This designation grants DIA special responsibilities for meeting intelligence requirements of the Secretary of Defense, Joint Chiefs, and Combatant Commanders. ([JP 2-0, Chapter III](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf)) As stated in JP 2-0, "DIA has oversight of the DIAP and is the DOD focal point for MASINT, HUMINT, and CI, and is the senior collection management authority for DOD within the IC, representing all Service and CCMD requirements for national collection."

**Defense MASINT Manager (DMM) role:** The Director, DIA, as DoD MASINT Manager, formally advises the Secretary of Defense and USD(I&S) on MASINT issues, establishes the National MASINT Requirements System, manages the USMS architecture, and fosters MASINT R&D through the National Consortium for MASINT Research (NCMR). ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) A separate **DoDI 3305.16** specifically assigns the Director, DIA, as the DoD MASINT training and certification functional manager. ([DoDI 3305.16, via DoDI 5105.58 reference](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf))

**Service MASINT cells:** The Secretaries of the Military Departments are required by DoDI 5105.58 to "designate an organizational entity within their respective Military Service as the Service MASINT focal point for all Service-related MASINT activities." Each Service MASINT cell must develop doctrine, tactics, techniques, procedures, and lessons-learned; facilitate federation and integration of Service MASINT tasking, collection, processing, exploitation, and dissemination; and program/provide military personnel to perform MASINT missions. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) The Air Force historically executes the largest share of MASINT funding and manpower under the General Defense Intelligence Program (GDIP). ([IC21 Report](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

**National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA) and Advanced Geospatial Intelligence (AGI):** While NGA's primary mission is GEOINT, it occupies a formal role in the MASINT enterprise. Under DoDI 5105.58, the Director, NGA, must support MASINT collection requirements that require GEOINT systems action, coordinate with DIA to develop integrated MASINT support, and provide GEOINT support to MASINT-related activities. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) Advanced Geospatial Intelligence (AGI) — the exploitation of spectral, polarimetric, and multi-look SAR phenomenologies — sits at the intersection of GEOINT and MASINT, with NGA holding primary exploitation responsibility. DoDI 5105.58 requires NGA to coordinate interoperability between the National System for Geospatial Intelligence and the U.S. MASINT System (USMS). ([DoDI 3115.15, GEOINT](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/311515p.pdf)) The Army's [MASINT/AGI Node factsheet](https://www.smdc.army.mil/Portals/38/Documents/Publications/Fact_Sheets/Archived_Fact_Sheets/MASINTNode.pdf) reflects the institutional co-location of MASINT and AGI exploitation at echelon.

**National Reconnaissance Office (NRO):** DoDI 5105.58 assigns the NRO responsibility for researching, developing, acquiring, launching, and maintaining the operational capability of national space-based MASINT collection systems. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf))

**Combatant Command MASINT Officer:** Under DoDI 5105.58, Commanders of the Combatant Commands must integrate, plan, and execute MASINT operations in support of approved plans; submit MASINT information requirements to DIA with information copies to MASCOM; exercise operational control of allocated military MASINT platforms; and apprise the Secretary of Defense and Director, DIA, of the operational status of all allocated MASINT resources. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) In practice, MASINT Officers embedded in Combatant Command J-2 staffs serve as the primary advocates, requirements generators, and liaisons for theater MASINT support.

## 4. Primary Collection Methods / Sub-Disciplines

Per the [ODNI MASINT Primer (2022)](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf) and the Center for MASINT Studies and Research at the Air Force Institute of Technology, MASINT is organized into **six major sub-disciplines**. These overlap and intertwine; a single collection event will frequently produce data relevant to multiple sub-disciplines. ([Wikipedia: Measurement and Signature Intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence))

---

### Electro-Optical (EO) MASINT

EO MASINT is "the collection of reflected or emitted energy across the optical portion (ultraviolet, visible, near-infrared, and infrared) of the electromagnetic spectrum." Data is collected by radiometers, spectrometers, non-imaging systems, lasers, and fiber optics. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

Three main sensor categories operate within EO MASINT:

- **Spectral sensors** measure frequency/wavelengths of radiation associated with specific targets or events, exploiting scattering, absorption, reflection, and emission properties.
- **Infrared (IR) sensors** measure changes in light waves and convert them into electromagnetic signals; they can track and characterize reentry vehicles on ballistic missile flight tests and Nuclear Detonations (NUDETs).
- **Laser collection** is formally defined in the ODNI Primer as "the interception, processing, and analysis of optical radiation to evaluate the technical characteristics and performance of the laser and its associated systems."

EO MASINT is distinct from traditional IMINT: while IMINT creates pictorial representations, EO MASINT performs spectral analysis of material content, energy emission, or thermal characteristics. Hyperspectral imaging (HSI) and multispectral imaging (MSI) fall within EO MASINT when exploitation seeks to characterize signatures rather than produce imagery products. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

---

### Geophysical MASINT

Geophysical MASINT is "the collection, processing, and exploitation of disturbances that affect the ionosphere, oceans and firmament of the Earth." It encompasses:

- **Seismic** — pressure waves and vibrations transmitted through the Earth's crust, capable of detecting underground explosions, tunnel construction, or vehicle movement
- **Acoustic** — sound propagation through air and water, including detection and classification of submarine propulsion signatures and torpedo launch events
- **Magnetic** — measurement of magnetic field anomalies caused by ferrous metallic objects such as submarines, vehicles, and buried ordnance
- **Gravimetric** — measurement of gravitational perturbations, relevant to detecting large underground voids or tunnels
- **Ionospheric** — sensing of disturbances in the upper atmosphere caused by high-altitude nuclear detonations or high-powered electromagnetic events

([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

Geophysical MASINT "aids the detection, classification, and surveillance of missiles, submarines, surface ships, ground vehicles, and aircraft" and can provide "indications and warning of troop and vehicle movements or nuclear explosions." ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf)) JP 2-0 Appendix B lists "acoustic intelligence" and "magnetic intelligence" as sub-categories of geophysical data collection. ([JP 2-0, Appendix B](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf))

---

### Materials MASINT

Materials MASINT involves "the physical collection, processing, and scientific analysis of gas, liquid, or solid samples" — collected from air, water, or solid material at or near a target location — and analyzed for isotopic, chemical, or biological signatures. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

For advanced materials, this sub-discipline explores ceramics, composites, organics, metallic materials, smart materials, and special materials for armor, weapons, and vehicle applications. The ODNI Primer notes that "material agents pose unique challenges for confirmatory analysis and conclusive attribution to their point of origin when found in the environment," making chain-of-custody requirements especially stringent. Chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear (CBRN) threat characterization is a primary mission of this sub-discipline. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

---

### Nuclear Radiation MASINT

Nuclear Radiation MASINT is "the active or passive detection, collection, and measurement of gamma rays, neutrons, x-rays, or other radiation phenomena from nuclear sources." Sensors include overhead satellites, hand-held radiation sensors at border crossings, and emplaced sensors for detection and identification of nuclear material transfers. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

This sub-discipline supports nuclear detonation detection (including post-detonation debris analysis), nuclear reactor effluent monitoring, weapons yield estimation, and treaty verification. JP 2-0 identifies "nuclear radiation data" as encompassing "nuclear radiation and physical phenomena associated with nuclear weapons, processes, materials, devices, or facilities." ([JP 2-0, Appendix B](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf)) The overlap with Materials MASINT is significant: while Nuclear Radiation MASINT focuses on direct ionization measurement (gamma, neutron, X-ray), Materials MASINT addresses the field or laboratory analysis of collected samples from air sampling, contaminated sites, and post-event debris. ([Nuclear MASINT, Knowino](https://www.theochem.ru.nl/~pwormer/Knowino/knowino.org/wiki/Nuclear_MASINT.html))

---

### Radar MASINT

Radar MASINT is "the active or passive collection of electromagnetic energy reflected from a target," providing "information on radar cross sections — tracking, precise measurements of components' size, shape, and motion characteristics for targets and objects." ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

Radar MASINT goes beyond traditional radar intelligence (RADINT) by exploiting **unintentional** characteristics of reflected returns: Doppler effects, polarimetric scattering matrices, line-of-sight signature measurements, and bistatic cross-sections. It includes direct (line-of-sight), indirect (bistatic), and over-the-horizon collection geometries. Analysts can extract radar cross-section (RCS) data to characterize platform shape, composition, and orientation — including the signature of stealth platforms or adversary aircraft under test. The IC21 report specifically identifies bistatic RF solutions as enabling "real-time non-cooperative target identification (NCTI)" and discriminating between real targets and radar decoys. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

---

### Radiofrequency (RF) MASINT

RF MASINT is "the collection, processing, and exploitation of electromagnetic radiation at frequencies from nearly zero up through TeraHertz (THz)." Primary source categories include:

- **Nuclear and simulated nuclear electromagnetic pulse (EMP)**
- **Intentionally radiated and collateral signals from Radio Frequency Weapons (RFW)** — RFW systems that use directed electromagnetic pulses to disable aircraft, munitions, satellites, air defense systems, and C4ISR electronics
- **Unintentionally Radiated Emissions (URE)** from diverse mechanical and electronic sources — engines, weapon systems, and electronics

([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

A primary objective is to "diagnose the technical parameters of a target device for the purposes of determining its power levels, operating characteristics, and signatures," enabling development of countermeasures and assessments of adversary advanced weapon technologies. JP 2-0 defines this data as "radio frequency/electromagnetic pulse emissions associated with nuclear testing, or other high energy events for the purpose of determining power levels, operating characteristics, and signatures of advanced technology weapons, power, and propulsion systems." ([JP 2-0, Appendix B](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf)) The overlap with SIGINT/FISINT is deliberate: telemetry and foreign instrumentation signals can be processed for either communications content (FISINT) or performance signature characterization (RF MASINT); the outcome, not the collection system, defines the discipline.

## 5. Signature / Indicator Catalog — What MASINT Sees

MASINT's multi-phenomenology reach enables collection against targets that resist characterization by IMINT or SIGINT alone. The following catalog reflects unclassified doctrine and publicly acknowledged collection programs.

**Missile Launches and Impacts (Electro-Optical / Radar / RF MASINT):**  
The **Space Based Infrared System (SBIRS)** provides 24/7 global strategic missile warning through scanning and step-staring sensors that detect IR signatures at boost phase. SBIRS sensors "gather raw, unprocessed data that are down-linked to the ground" and also perform on-board signal processing, supporting missile early warning, missile defense, battlespace awareness, and technical intelligence missions. ([USSF SBIRS Fact Sheet](https://www.spaceforce.mil/about-us/fact-sheets/article/2197746/space-based-infrared-system/)) The **RC-135S Cobra Ball** (operated by Air Force Global Strike Command's 45th Reconnaissance Squadron and 55th Wing) gathers EO/MASINT data on ballistic missile signatures during boost and reentry phases using the Medium Wave Infrared Array (MIRA) sensor suite, all-weather tracking radar, and the Real Time Optics System (RTOS) plus Large Aperture Tracker System (LATS). Data is "critical to arms treaty compliance verification, and development of U.S. strategic defense and theater missile defense concepts." ([RC-135S Fact Sheet, Offutt AFB](https://www.offutt.af.mil/Resources/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/1748019/rc-135s-cobra-ball/); [IRP FAS Cobra Ball](https://irp.fas.org/program/collect/cobra_ball.htm))

**Nuclear Detonation Signatures (Nuclear Radiation / Geophysical MASINT):**  
Space-based nuclear sensors — originated with the **Vela Hotel** satellite series beginning in 1959 — use X-ray, neutron, and gamma-ray detectors, as well as optical bhangmeters that detect the characteristic double-flash light signature of nuclear bursts milliseconds apart. ([Nuclear MASINT, Knowino](https://www.theochem.ru.nl/~pwormer/Knowino/knowino.org/wiki/Nuclear_MASINT.html)) Ground-based seismic networks, coordinated through the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty Organization (CTBTO) International Monitoring System, provide independent nuclear explosion detection through seismic, acoustic, and radionuclide techniques. ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence)) The detection of the Soviet Union's first atomic test in September 1949 by a WB-29 flying between Alaska and Japan — six months before the U.S. expected it — validated the strategic importance of atmospheric collection. ([USAF WC-135 Fact Sheet](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104494/wc-135-constant-phoenix/))

**CBRN Release Detection (Materials / Nuclear MASINT):**  
The **WC-135 Constant Phoenix** atmospheric collection aircraft — operated by the 45th Reconnaissance Squadron and supported by Detachment 1, Air Force Technical Applications Center — collects particulate and gaseous effluents using external flow-through devices and whole-air collection spheres. The on-board atmospheric collection suite allows mission crews to detect radioactive clouds in real time using a Directional Gamma Sensor System (DGSS), Radiation Monitoring and Analysis System (RMAS), and whole-air collection system. It supports the Limited Nuclear Test Ban Treaty of 1963 and detected radioactive debris from North Korea's October 2006 nuclear test and Fukushima reactor emissions in March 2011. ([USAF WC-135 Fact Sheet](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104494/wc-135-constant-phoenix/); [Wikipedia WC-135](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boeing_WC-135_Constant_Phoenix))

**Submarine Acoustic Signatures (Geophysical MASINT):**  
The **T-AGOS/SURTASS** (Surveillance Towed Array Sensor System) platform combines a passive towed horizontal hydrophone array (TL-29A, approximately 1,000 feet long) with an optional Low Frequency Active (LFA) active sonar component. It is employed to detect nuclear and diesel submarines at long ranges, providing detection, classification, and tracking data to theater ASW commanders through the Integrated Undersea Surveillance System. ([DOT&E SURTASS/CLFA Report, FY2015](https://www.dote.osd.mil/Portals/97/pub/reports/FY2015/navy/2015surtass_clfa.pdf)) The ODNI MASINT Primer specifically identifies geophysical MASINT as providing "vital information and detailed signatures of threat submarine platforms, signatures from operation of submarine-launched ballistic missile systems, and other sea-launched weapons." ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

**Tunneling / Underground Construction (Geophysical / Gravimetric MASINT):**  
Seismic and gravimetric MASINT techniques can detect and characterize underground facility construction activity. The ODNI Primer lists "underground facilities construction activity" among geophysical MASINT signature sources, including the detection of vibrations, pressure waves, and gravitational perturbations associated with heavy excavation or tunneling. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf)) A JASON/DoD study on [characterization of underground facilities (1999)](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/ugf.pdf) identified standoff methods including SAR change detection, Fraunhofer hyperspectral imaging, and laser vibrometry for above-ground evidence of subsurface activity.

**Camouflage-Defeating Spectral Signatures (EO MASINT — Hyperspectral):**  
Hyperspectral imaging (HSI) captures reflected radiation across hundreds of spectral bands, enabling identification of specific materials within a scanned area. HSI exploits the fact that plastic synthetic camouflage materials possess spectral signatures distinctly different from natural vegetation, allowing analysts to identify deployed camouflage even when it visually matches its surroundings. ([Saab Barracuda analysis, 2024](https://www.saab.com/newsroom/stories/2024/october/emerging-solutions-to-beat-the-hyperspectral-threat)) The DoD MASINT Evaluation Report notes that MASINT can "ascertain the presence of operational equipment under camouflage" as a key tactical application. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

**Disturbed Earth (HSI/MSI):**  
EO MASINT hyperspectral and multispectral sensors can detect recently turned earth through spectral differences in soil moisture content, mineral composition, and vegetation stress. This capability supports detection of recently laid mines, shallow graves, improvised explosive device (IED) emplacement, and cache sites. Spectrometry can differentiate between surface materials at the sub-pixel level.

**Vehicle / Equipment Thermal Signatures (EO MASINT — IR):**  
Infrared sensors detect residual heat signatures from engines, exhaust, and equipment use — allowing analysts to determine whether vehicles are actively operating or have recently operated, estimate force composition by thermal cross-sections, and identify decoys that lack genuine thermal signatures. MASINT can achieve "unambiguous discrimination for identification of friend and foe" and reduce fratricide risk through thermal signature matching. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

**Stealth Platform Measurement (Radar MASINT):**  
Radar cross-section (RCS) measurement — including bistatic and multistatic collection geometries — enables characterization of low-observable ("stealth") platforms. Bistatic collection exploits the fact that even low-RCS platforms scatter radar energy in directions away from the transmitter, and can detect aircraft that near-monostatic systems miss. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

**Telemetry and Instrumentation (RF MASINT / FISINT overlap):**  
Foreign instrumentation signals — telemetry, beacon data, and engineering measurements transmitted from ballistic missiles, space launch vehicles, and advanced weapons during test and evaluation — sit at the intersection of RF MASINT and Foreign Instrumentation Signals Intelligence (FISINT). The RC-135S Cobra Ball collects both optical and electronic data simultaneously; when processed for performance characterization, the result is RF/EO MASINT; when processed for communications content, it falls under FISINT. ([IRP FAS Cobra Ball](https://irp.fas.org/program/collect/cobra_ball.htm)) JP 2-0 addresses "weapon system intelligence" and "scientific intelligence" as outputs derived in part from this overlap. ([JP 2-0, Appendix B](https://www.usna.edu/Training/_files/documents/References/2C%20MQS%20References/2015-2016%20MQS/Joint%20Publication%20JP%202-0%20Joint%20Intelligence.pdf))

**Foliage Penetration (Radar / Multispectral MASINT):**  
Very low frequency radar (VHF-band or lower) can penetrate forest canopy to detect vehicles, personnel, or structures underneath. This was demonstrated in the Vietnam-era context and continues to inform airborne and space-based sensor development. Multispectral sensors can also detect spectral anomalies caused by foreign objects concealed under vegetation, complementing foliage-penetration radar.

## 6. PED Chain

The MASINT processing, exploitation, and dissemination (PED) chain is the most technically demanding and resource-intensive in the IC, reflecting the scientific depth required to convert raw sensor data into finished intelligence.

**Collection:**  
MASINT data is acquired from a variety of satellite, airborne, shipborne, and ground-based platforms, as well as remotely piloted vehicles. The U.S. MASINT System (USMS), managed by NMMO under Director, DIA, provides "the framework for tasking, collection, processing, exploitation and dissemination and research and development activities." ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) The NRO manages national space-based MASINT collection systems; Military Departments maintain airborne and surface collection assets. The National MASINT Requirements System is a web-based application that supports creation, submission, and tracking of MASINT requirements. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf))

**Specialized Exploitation by the DIA Defense MASINT Enterprise:**  
Because MASINT signatures require specialized scientific and engineering knowledge for proper interpretation, exploitation is centralized at DIA's science and technology directorate level. AFDP 2-0 notes that "DIA leads the IC effort for MASINT via senior science and technology officers (SSTOs) who integrate DIA efforts to meet operational intelligence requirements." ([AFDP 2-0](https://www.doctrine.af.mil/Portals/61/documents/AFDP_2-0/2-0-AFDP-INTELLIGENCE.pdf)) The CMO-3 (Central MASINT Operations Center) historically planned, coordinated, and executed MASINT collection operations, serving as the focal point for time-sensitive requirements. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

**NGA AGI Exploitation:**  
For spectral, polarimetric SAR, and other geospatially-oriented MASINT phenomenologies, NGA performs AGI exploitation. DoDI 5105.58 requires NGA to "support MASINT collection and data requirements that require GEOINT systems action" and to "coordinate with the Director, DIA, to develop and provide integrated support to MASINT and MASINT-related activities." The [JASON/DoD Characterization of Underground Facilities study](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/ugf.pdf) reflects the overlap where NGA's SAR and spectral exploitation informs MASINT assessments of subsurface activity.

**Service MASINT Cells:**  
Each military Service maintains a designated MASINT focal point with limited but operationally significant exploitation capacity, focused on Service-specific platforms and the tactical translation of MASINT products for warfighters. Under DoDI 5105.58, Service cells must "orchestrate Service MASINT tasking, collection, processing, production, exploitation, and dissemination functions." ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) However, the IC21 evaluation found that 49 percent of collection managers at the war-fighting level had a poor understanding of MASINT, limiting the tactical utility of Service-level exploitation. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

**Long PED Timelines:**  
Many MASINT sub-disciplines involve forensic-grade analysis that inherently requires extended timelines. Materials MASINT requires laboratory processing of collected samples for isotopic and chemical analysis; nuclear debris analysis requires rigorous chain-of-custody and certified measurement procedures. The IC21 study identified timeliness as the primary reason warfighters found MASINT tactically limited: 71 percent of Combatant Command respondents and 60 percent of tactical commanders cited untimely data as the key constraint, with "timely" defined as products received within 24–48 hours. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm)) Near-real-time MASINT products (e.g., SBIRS missile warning, acoustic detections) exist but are the exception rather than the rule across the discipline.

**The National MASINT Data Broker** and **MASINT Portal** were developed under NMMO to provide federated dissemination of MASINT products, central discovery, advanced data-mining, and IC-wide access to MASINT signatures and tools. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf))

## 7. Exploitation Tradecraft (Unclassified, Doctrinal)

**Signature Libraries:**  
The core tradecraft artifact of MASINT is the **signature library**: a repository of measured, characterized, and catalogued physical properties of known targets and events. Automatic target recognition (ATR) requires "extensive signature libraries and reliable matching to them." ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence)) MASINT "measurement searches for differences from known norms, and characterizes the signatures of new phenomena" — when a new rocket fuel exhaust is first measured, it becomes a new signature in the MASINT database. ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence)) The ODNI MASINT Primer's definition of a signature as "a unique set of characteristics that differentiate one item or event from another" underscores the library's role as the discriminant basis for all subsequent collection exploitation. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf)) DoDI 5105.58 requires NMMO to "prescribe and mandate MASINT standards to ensure interoperability; validate the integration of standards and architectures into the USMS; and support information sharing, advanced data-mining, and IC access to MASINT products, data, tools, and signatures." ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf))

**Change Detection Across Phenomenologies:**  
A primary analytical technique is multi-temporal change detection — comparing signatures from the same target or area at different times to identify activity, new construction, changed operational status, or degradation. In the MASINT Handbook for the Warfighter context, exploitable MASINT processing includes "change detection, terrain mapping, underwater obstacle detection, dynamic imaging of moving targets, and radar cross-section signature measurements." ([MASINT Handbook for the Warfighter, redacted, via FAS](https://irp.fas.org/dia/masint-1994.pdf)) In the geophysical domain, change detection can reveal new tunneling or underground activity by detecting changes in seismic background noise; in the spectral domain, it can detect disturbed earth or vegetation stress.

**Cross-Phenomenology Fusion:**  
Exploitation tradecraft emphasizes fusing data from multiple phenomenologies against the same target. The former CMO Director Barbara Sanderson described cross-cueing as "a key approach" involving "fusing data from several different types of sensors to detect, locate and identify a target." ([AFCEA SIGNAL Magazine, 2002](https://www.afcea.org/signal-media/intelligence/low-technology-foes-require-high-technology-detection)) The wikipedia MASINT article formalizes this: "A fast sensitive sensor covering a large area, such as SIGINT or acoustic, can pass coordinates of a target of interest to a sensitive narrowband RF spectrum analyzer for ELINT or a hyperspectral electro-optical sensor." ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence)) For an artillery target, electro-optical and radar sensors could determine muzzle velocity while chemical and spectroscopic sensors could characterize the propellant — fusing physical, kinematic, and chemical signatures into a single definitive target characterization.

**Calibration and Ground Truth Requirements:**  
Because MASINT produces quantitative measurements, calibration is a fundamental tradecraft requirement. Collection systems must be calibrated against known physical standards before their measurements can be used to build signature libraries or discriminate among targets. For overhead spectral sensors, this requires atmospheric correction to remove the effects of scattering and absorption. For nuclear sensors, calibration against certified reference sources is required. Ground truth — the correlation of remotely sensed signatures with physically confirmed or laboratory-analyzed samples — is essential for validating new signature library entries.

**Forensic Standards and Chain of Custody:**  
For Materials MASINT and Nuclear Radiation MASINT, forensic-grade exploitation requires strict chain-of-custody documentation. The ODNI Primer notes that "material agents pose unique challenges for confirmatory analysis and conclusive attribution to their point of origin when found in the environment." ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf)) DoDI 5105.58 directs DIA to "foster MASINT support to forensics, biometrics, and identity management activities," reflecting the convergence of MASINT tradecraft with the broader forensic intelligence enterprise. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) In treaty verification contexts (CTBT, New START), chain-of-custody requirements for atmospheric collection samples rise to an internationally adjudicated evidentiary standard.

**Imagery-Defined MASINT (IDM):**  
A specialized tradecraft methodology: rather than using an image as a picture, MASINT analysts apply it as a measurement matrix. "In IDM, a MASINT application would measure the image, pixel by pixel, and try to identify the physical materials, or types of energy, that are responsible for pixels or groups of pixels: signatures. When the signatures are then correlated to precise geography, or details of an object, the combined information becomes something greater than the whole of its IMINT and MASINT parts." ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence))

## 8. Strengths and Limitations

### Strengths

**Defeats Camouflage, Concealment, and Deception (CCD):**  
MASINT exploits the physical properties *intrinsic* to an object — its material composition, thermal emissions, acoustic signature, radar cross-section, or isotopic content — rather than its visual appearance. An adversary can camouflage a vehicle to defeat optical sensors but cannot eliminate its IR heat signature after engine operation, its radar cross-section, or its acoustic emissions. The ODNI Primer states: "MASINT's focus on scientific measurement of the quantitative and qualitative information inherent to the object or activity makes MASINT difficult to deceive." ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf)) Radar decoys can fool IMINT; MASINT can recognize them "because the signal lacks unintentional characteristics of the real radar system." ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence))

**Definitive Technical Characterization:**  
MASINT can provide "specific weapon system identifications, chemical compositions and material content, and a potential adversary's ability to employ these weapons" — exactly the information that policymakers need for treaty verification, WMD attribution, and weapons system assessment. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html)) Unlike IMINT — which shows presence — MASINT shows what a target *is* and how it *performs*.

**Deception-Resistant for Many Signatures:**  
Many physical signatures are involuntary and unavoidable. A nuclear reactor emits characteristic radionuclides whether or not it is camouflaged; a ballistic missile leaves an infrared plume at boost phase regardless of its color scheme; a submarine propeller emits acoustic cavitation regardless of surface appearance. The IC21 report noted that MASINT "often, these contributions are the first indicators of hostile activities." ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

**Supports Treaty Verification and Non-Proliferation:**  
MASINT provides the technical underpinning for arms control verification, including atmospheric sampling under the LTBT, seismic monitoring under the CTBT, and ballistic missile performance characterization for treaty compliance. ([ODNI MASINT Primer, 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/21-113_MASINT_Primer__2022.pdf))

### Limitations

**Highly Specialized PED, Slow Timelines:**  
"MASINT is a science-intensive discipline that needs people/scientists well versed in the broad range of physical and electrical sciences." ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html)) This requirement creates chronic PED capacity constraints. The 1997 DoD IG evaluation found that timeliness was the primary operational limitation: warfighters rated MASINT as tactically useful only when products arrived within 24–48 hours, but forensic sub-disciplines inherently require longer processing. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

**Narrow Targets, Require Baseline Signature Libraries:**  
MASINT is most effective against targets that have been previously characterized and entered into signature libraries. Against novel targets or events for which no signature baseline exists, exploitation is severely degraded. The IC21 report found that "MASINT has no formal/viable method (i.e., metrics) for evaluating MASINT contributions." ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))

**Few Collectors, Scarce National Capacity:**  
The IC21 report and the FAS MASINT Evaluation Report both document chronic under-resourcing. The Air Force executes approximately 84 percent of GDIP MASINT funding and 93 percent of associated manpower. ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html)) The CMO had only 30 authorized DIA billets (with 27 filled) at the time of the 1997 evaluation, compared to the 75–100 recommended by IC respondents. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm)) This "scarce capacity" dynamic remains a structural feature of the discipline.

**Often National-Only:**  
The most capable MASINT collection systems — space-based nuclear detection satellites, SBIRS, the WC-135 Constant Phoenix, the RC-135S Cobra Ball, and the national-level seismic monitoring network — are national-level assets operated under NRO, Space Force, or Air Force Global Strike Command authority. Tasking requires going through DIA/NMMO, MASCOM, and the CJCS; CCMD-level authorities generally cannot independently task national MASINT assets. This creates a tension between national collection priorities and theater intelligence requirements. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf))

**Identity Crisis and User Education Gap:**  
MASINT "is the least understood of the disciplines." ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html)) In the 1997 evaluation, 51 percent of warfighters with MASINT responsibilities had received no formal MASINT training, and 49 percent of collection managers at the operational level had poor understanding of MASINT products. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm)) This translates into underdeveloped requirements, missed collection opportunities, and poor constituency for MASINT budget advocacy.

## 9. Cross-Cueing Patterns

Cross-cueing — the passing of detection, geolocation, and targeting information among sensors and disciplines — is central to maximizing MASINT's contribution to the all-source intelligence picture. ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence)) MASINT both generates cues for other disciplines and receives cues from them.

**MASINT → IMINT (Signature Anomaly Cues Imagery):**  
When an EO spectral or thermal MASINT sensor detects an anomalous signature — disturbed earth, unusual thermal emission, or spectral-band departure from background — it can cue tasking of high-resolution imagery to confirm the target. The former CMO Director explicitly cited how "MASINT data can help direct other sensors to close in on a target" and that cross-cueing "proved the worth of MASINT" in Operation Enduring Freedom. ([AFCEA SIGNAL Magazine, 2002](https://www.afcea.org/signal-media/intelligence/low-technology-foes-require-high-technology-detection)) Imagery-Defined MASINT (IDM) reverses this: IMINT collections become inputs to MASINT spectral analysis, with the fused product exceeding what either discipline alone could produce. ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence))

**MASINT → SIGINT (Telemetry Intercept Cues SIGINT/FISINT):**  
RF MASINT sensors can detect and characterize unintentional emissions from weapon systems in test, cueing SIGINT/FISINT collectors to confirm and expand coverage. The overlap between RF MASINT and FISINT around ballistic missile telemetry is institutional: the RC-135S collects both; the distinction lies in whether the product exploits performance signatures (MASINT) or communications content (FISINT). ([IRP FAS Cobra Ball](https://irp.fas.org/program/collect/cobra_ball.htm)) SIGINT systems also produce "incidental radar characteristics like spillover of the main beam (side lobes) or transmitter interference" that can be re-processed as MASINT. ([Wikipedia MASINT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurement_and_signature_intelligence))

**SBIRS → Space Situational Awareness + Missile Warning:**  
SBIRS data simultaneously supports missile warning (tactical cue to Patriot and THAAD batteries), missile defense (track data for kill vehicle guidance), battlespace awareness (characterization of launch location, trajectory, likely impact area), and technical intelligence (collection of boost-phase IR signature for the MASINT library). ([USSF SBIRS Fact Sheet](https://www.spaceforce.mil/about-us/fact-sheets/article/2197746/space-based-infrared-system/); [USSPACECOM SBIRS article](https://www.spacecom.mil/Newsroom/News/Article-Display/Article/3130787/sbirs-geo-6-launch-closes-out-two-decades-of-progress-in-missile-warning-tracki/)) The RC-135S Cobra Ball can subsequently provide on-scene EO reconnaissance and within seconds of launch detection provide "estimates of launch point, intercept point, and impact point" for transmission to theater C4I, demonstrating the MASINT → tactical warning → targeting cue chain. ([IRP FAS Cobra Ball](https://irp.fas.org/program/collect/cobra_ball.htm))

**Acoustic MASINT → Maritime Targeting:**  
SURTASS/T-AGOS passive acoustic detection provides initial submarine contact data — including bearing, range, and preliminary acoustic signature classification — to theater ASW commanders. This acoustic MASINT cue then drives prosecution by maritime patrol aircraft (P-8 Poseidon), attack submarines, and surface combatants. Geophysical MASINT from T-AGOS "provides passive detection of nuclear and diesel submarines and enables real-time reporting of surveillance information to ASW commanders." ([DOT&E SURTASS/CLFA FY2015](https://www.dote.osd.mil/Portals/97/pub/reports/FY2015/navy/2015surtass_clfa.pdf)) The acoustic signature characterization from geophysical MASINT also feeds the signature library, enabling future identification of the same submarine class.

**HSI/MSI → Defeat of CCD:**  
Hyperspectral and multispectral EO MASINT systematically defeats camouflage, concealment, and deception (CCD) by detecting material-level spectral differences that visual or broadband sensors cannot resolve. As Saab's defense analysis notes, "a multispectral sensor is likely to be fooled by a camouflage system whose colour, reflective properties and thermal properties match its surrounds," while a hyperspectral sensor can identify the presence of plastic synthetic materials in the camouflage — cueing IMINT tasking to confirm and characterize the hidden asset. ([Saab Barracuda, October 2024](https://www.saab.com/newsroom/stories/2024/october/emerging-solutions-to-beat-the-hyperspectral-threat)) The DoD MASINT Evaluation Report confirms: MASINT can "ascertain the presence of operational equipment under camouflage" and "identify quantities and types of fuels in fuel storage areas" — both products that directly cue imagery and targeting. ([FAS MASINT Evaluation Report](https://irp.fas.org/program/masint_evaluation_rep.htm))

**Multi-Discipline Fusion as the Ideal:**  
The ODNI MASINT Primer and IC doctrine consistently emphasize that MASINT's highest value is achieved through cross-discipline integration, not independent operation. DoDI 5105.58 explicitly requires the NMMO to "foster MASINT support for geospatial intelligence (GEOINT) and signals intelligence (SIGINT) activities" and requires NGA and NSA to achieve interoperability with MASINT architectures and standards. ([DoDI 5105.58](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/DD/issuances/dodi/510558p.pdf)) The IC21 report recommended that "MASINT should remain a specific collection and processing discipline" while traditional disciplines must "specifically address, in their charters, the recognition of the MASINT ability to glean additional data from their collections." ([IC21 Report, Chapter VII](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-IC21/html/GPO-IC21-7.html))
